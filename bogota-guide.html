Orientation

What to know before you move

The essentials, up front: money, the rate, how people actually talk, and how to stay sharp. Bogotá rewards confidence and quiet awareness in equal measure.

Currency

Colombian Peso

COP · symbol $ (often written $20.000 = 20,000 pesos)

Exchange Rate

≈ 3,430

1 USD ≈ 3,420–3,450 COP (late June 2026). Drop three zeros and triple it for a rough USD figure.

Altitude

2,640 m

≈8,660 ft. Expect harder breathing on your first 2–3 runs. It's the elevation, not your fitness. Hydrate aggressively.

Getting Around

Uber / Didi

Both work and are the safe default. Sit up front so it reads as a friend's car. Never hail a yellow street cab.

Staying sharp — safety

  • "No dar papaya" is the local rule: don't flash phone, watch, or cash on the street.
  • Your zone (Chicó / Parque 93 / Zona T) is among the safest — walk it freely by day.
  • Use Uber/Didi after dark rather than walking long stretches, even in the north.
  • La Candelaria is fine in daylight on busy streets; don't wander empty side-streets.
  • Withdraw cash from ATMs inside malls or banks, not street machines.
  • Carry a card + small cash; leave passport in the safe, a photo on your phone.
  • Tap water in the north is generally safe to drink; bottled is the easy default.
  • Emergency line: 123. Tourist police are present around Zona T.

03 · The Afternoons

One neat experience a day

You've done a volcano, chocolate and cooking classes lately — so here's a progression of distinct, hands-on afternoons, building from light to genuinely memorable.

Signature

Emerald ring-making

Small private workshop · book ahead

Carve and set your own emerald ring and walk out wearing it. Colombia is the world's largest emerald exporter — nobody tells you to do this, and it's the one you'll show off for years.

Book early via Viator / GetYourGuide; spots are limited.

Culture

Graffiti tour, La Candelaria

Historic centre

Bogotá is a world graffiti capital; a guide untangles the tags and politics you'd walk straight past. Pair with Prudencia for lunch and the Gold Museum.

Daylight only, busy streets. Mid-stay is ideal.

Food

Paloquemao fruit tour

Plaza de Mercado

A guide walks you through pyramids of exotic fruit — try lulo (best as juice) and spiky guanabana. Among the best-rated food experiences in the city. Go earlier in the day.

Golden hour

Monserrate at sunset

3,150 m mountain church

Funicular or cable car up for the full-city panorama. Given your base you could hike it — but sunset is the point.

Athletic & absurd

Tejo

Local cancha + beer

Colombia's national sport: hurl clay discs at gunpowder targets that explode on a hit. Ridiculous, social, fantastic.

Half-day out

Lake Guatavita

~1.5–2 hr NE

The sacred Muisca lake behind the El Dorado legend — a serene escape if you want a longer afternoon with a story attached.

04 · The Weekend

Villa de Leyva & your 14-mile Sunday

The right call over Zipaquirá: a whitewashed colonial town with one of South America's largest plazas, a near-desert microclimate, vineyards, and quiet runnable terrain that makes your long run a pleasure.

Hire a driver — don't rent

It's a 3–4 hr drive (156 km) on narrow, curvy mountain roads with aggressive overtaking, plus Pico y Placa plate restrictions that can bar a rental on a given day. A private round-trip driver lets you nap, work, and arrive fresh. Stop at the Zipaquirá Salt Cathedral on the way up.

The shape of the weekend

Saturday AM Drive up with the Zipaquirá Salt Cathedral stop; arrive Villa de Leyva early afternoon. Plaza Mayor, Ain Karim vineyard, the Terracotta House.
Sunday — the 14-miler Run early from the plaza toward Pozos Azules (turquoise desert pools) and the El Fósil road — quiet, scenic, ~600 m lower than Bogotá so you'll breathe easier. Dry climate, highs ~22°C / lows ~12°C. Front-load hydration; have your driver crew a turnaround.
Sunday PM Lunch, then the relaxed drive back to Bogotá.

Alt for a tougher run: Suesca (~1 hr out) offers trail and rock-country routes — but Villa de Leyva is the stronger all-around weekend.

05 · Right Now

Tonight, and tomorrow after the run

Tomorrow is a Sunday — which is perfect timing. Two of Bogotá's best things happen only on Sundays, and they bracket your day beautifully.

Tonight · chill

Salvo Patria or Mesa Franca

Chapinero · 8–12 min ride

Low-key, excellent, no-fuss. Salvo Patria for garden-table warmth and natural wine; Mesa Franca for creative small plates at the bar. Either is the easy, unpretentious first night you want after travel.

Walk-in friendly earlier; book if after 8pm.

Tomorrow night · fancy

El Chato

Chapinero Alto · ~12 min

Your marquee dinner — #1 in Latin America. Reserve the upstairs tasting menu at the glass-walled kitchen. If El Chato can't seat you, pivot to Leo. Book this the moment you read this.

A week+ lead is normal; have your host call.

Tomorrow (Sunday), after your run

Morning — La Ciclovía Over 120 km of city roads close to cars 7am–2pm every Sunday. This is a safe, scenic, flat run venue right outside your door — the first program of its kind in the world.
Recovery coffee Roll into Azahar or Libertario at Parque 93 — both walkable from home — for a long flat white and pastries.
Afternoon — Usaquén Sunday market The colonial-village quarter turns into an open-air artisan fair: live music, local makers, boho-chic strolling. The single best Sunday-afternoon move in the city, ~12 min north.
Then — Monserrate at golden hour If you've got legs left, cap the day with the sunset panorama before your fancy dinner.
