Orientation

What to know before you move

The essentials, up front: the peso, the blue dollar rate, how people actually talk, and how to stay sharp. Buenos Aires rewards confidence and quiet local knowledge in equal measure — especially at the money changer.

Currency

Argentine Peso

ARS · symbol $ (written $1.500 = 1,500 pesos). Drop three zeros for a rough dollar figure at the official rate.

Official Rate

≈ 1,422

1 USD ≈ 1,420–1,450 ARS at banks and ATMs (mid-2026). This is what you get from any standard exchange.

Blue Dollar Rate

≈ 1,514

The parallel market rate (late June 2026) — about 6–7% better than official. Use licensed casas de cambio in Palermo, never street changers.

Altitude & Climate

Sea Level

July is mid-winter: highs ~14°C / 57°F, lows ~7°C / 44°F. Crisp, clear mornings — ideal for running. No acclimatisation needed whatsoever.

Getting Around

Uber / Cabify

Both work reliably and are the safe default. SUBE card (buy at kioscos) for subway (Subte) and buses. Never hail unmarked street cabs.

Running Base

Bosques de Palermo

980-acre park 5 min walk from Palermo Soho. Paved + unpaved circuits, lakeside paths, good surfaces. Best city-centre ultra training base in South America.

Talk like a Porteño — Buenos Aires slang

Che — Hey / mate (as in Guevara — it's literally just "hey")
Boludo/a — Between close friends: "dude / bro." To strangers: a real insult. Read the room.
Copado/a — Cool, awesome, great
Pinta — Looks good / vibe ("tiene buena pinta" = looks delicious)
Quilombo — Chaos, mess, commotion
Laburo — Work / job
Re- — Prefix meaning "very" (re-copado = very cool)
¿Todo bien? — Everything good? Standard street greeting
Trucho/a — Fake, dodgy, counterfeit
Morfi / Morfar — Food / to eat
Pibe / Piba — Young guy / young woman
Vos — "You" (replaces "tú" in Argentine Spanish)
Mina / Tipo — Woman / guy (casual)
Canchero — Suave, self-assured, cool about it

Staying sharp — safety

  • Palermo Soho and Hollywood are among Buenos Aires' safest barrios — walk freely by day and early evening.
  • Be alert in San Telmo after midnight, especially around Plaza Dorrego once the tourist crowd thins.
  • La Boca: visit Caminito by day only and do not wander off the tourist corridor — full stop.
  • Don't use your phone visibly while walking; pockets over handbags for both of you.
  • Use Uber or Cabify after dark — reliable, traceable, priced in pesos on your card at the official rate.
  • Blue dollar exchanges: licensed casas de cambio in Palermo or "cuevas" your host recommends. Never street changers ("¿cambio, cambio?").
  • ATMs dispense pesos at the official rate; withdraw from bank-lobby machines, not street-facing ones.
  • Medical emergency: Hospital Británico de Buenos Aires (Perdriel 74) is the go-to private hospital for English-speaking travellers.
  • Emergencies: 911 (police/all), 107 (ambulance), 100 (fire).
  • Tap water in Palermo is technically safe but many locals drink filtered or bottled. Your call.

03 · The Afternoons

One neat experience a day

Fifteen days means fifteen afternoons — here are nine distinct experiences that each deliver something the city does like nowhere else. Ordered from lightest to most memorable, and including one you'd only know about as a local.

Signature · must-do

Private tango lesson + milonga

Estudio DNI Tango or El Beso · book ahead

One private lesson in the afternoon ($60–80 pp), then show up for the beginner milonga at La Catedral (Sarmiento 4006) at 11pm — three levels, all welcome, live orchestra some nights. The Buenos Aires experience you can't skip. Brooke leads.

La Catedral charges ~$15 ARS entry. Book the lesson on Viator or direct with DNI Tango.

Culture

MALBA — Latin American Art Museum

Av. Figueroa Alcorta 3415 · Palermo · 10 min walk

Frida Kahlo, Diego Rivera, Tarsila do Amaral — the finest Latin American art museum on the continent, and it's practically on your doorstep in Palermo. Go on a weekday morning for empty rooms. The building itself is stunning.

Market

Feria de San Telmo (Sundays only)

Plaza Dorrego, San Telmo

One of South America's great Sunday markets — antiques, leather, vintage silver maté sets, live tango, street food. Arrives at 10am, peaks at noon. Pair with coffee at El Federal and lunch at Fogón Asado for the perfect Sunday.

Sunday only, 10am–5pm. Goes rain or shine.

Athletic

Reserva Ecológica Costanera Sur

Av. Tristán Achával Rodríguez 1550 · Puerto Madero

A 350-hectare urban nature reserve on the Río de la Plata — capybara, 300 bird species, river views. Run the perimeter for ~10km of gravel and dirt trail for a genuine escape from the city without leaving it. Sunrise or sunset are the moves.

Neighbourhood

La Boca / Caminito colour walk

La Boca neighbourhood

The corrugated-iron rainbow houses of Caminito street — best seen in an early afternoon when the light is warm and photographers haven't yet taken over. Take a cab to and from; don't walk the neighbourhood perimeter. Stop at a parrilla on El Caminito itself.

Daytime only on the tourist strip. Do not wander toward the port.

Architecture

Recoleta Cemetery + neighbourhood stroll

Junín 1760 · Recoleta

Evita's tomb, marble mausoleums, and the city's finest 19th-century funerary architecture in one walled city. Free to enter. Pair with the nearby MALBA-adjacent galleries and an Alvear Avenue walk for a refined afternoon out.

Day trip

Tigre Delta half-day

Tigre · 45 min train from Retiro

Take the Tren de la Costa from Retiro, then a launch (low wooden river boat) up the Paraná Delta — a labyrinth of islands, rustic restaurants, and zero cars. Lunch at one of the riverside parrillas; back by 6pm. The city's best nearby escape.

Launches depart Tigre station pier. No booking needed in winter.

Hidden gem

Florería Atlántico at night

Arroyo 872 · Retiro · 18 min

The flower shop closes, a secret door opens into the basement, and you're in one of the world's 50 best bars. Gin-focused, Latin botanicals, extraordinary cocktail theatre. The move for a special night that doesn't involve food. No reservation needed but go before midnight.

Palermo ritual

Bosques de Palermo — 10K shakeout

Parque Tres de Febrero · 5 min walk

On recovery days, run the park circuit through the Rosedal (8,000 roses in winter — bare but beautiful), past the lake, and back. 5–10km of varied paved + dirt surface. The city's best built-in training base. No gear needed beyond shoes.

04 · The Weekend

Colonia del Sacramento & your 17-mile Sunday

The obvious and correct call: a 1h15m Buquebus ferry across the Río de la Plata to Uruguay's prettiest town — UNESCO-listed cobblestones, Portuguese lighthouse, and riverside paths perfectly suited to a long run in cool winter air.

Ferry, not flight — it's that close

Buquebus departs from Puerto Madero (15 min Uber from Palermo). Fast crossing is about 45 min; regular crossing 1h15m. Buy tickets 5–7 days ahead — take your passport, this is a border crossing. Colonia Express and Seacat Colonia also operate the route at lower prices. Round-trip on Buquebus runs ~USD $110–170 per person.

The shape of the weekend

Saturday — Early ferry Catch the 8am or 9am crossing from Puerto Madero. Arrive Colonia before 11am. Drop bags at your hotel (Sheraton Colonia or Posada del Gobernador for a luxury night). Walk the UNESCO Historic Quarter: cobblestones, lighthouse, old city wall, Plaza Mayor. Afternoon wine at Charco restaurant on the riverfront.
Sunday — The 17-miler Start at 6:30am before the town wakes. Run the Rambla (riverside promenade) east for 4 miles, then take the Camino Real road into the countryside — flat to gently rolling, quiet asphalt, cool 10–12°C air. Turn around at mile 8.5 and come back via the interior. Bring your own hydration for miles 6–12; Brooke can crew the midpoint. Winter air at sea level = you'll feel like a machine. Cooldown along the waterfront.
Sunday afternoon Post-run breakfast at Pulpería de los Faroles. Wander the antique shops on Calle de los Suspiros. Ferry back at 5pm or 7pm — you'll be in Palermo in time for a quiet dinner at home or El Preferido.

For a bigger run weekend: Book two nights and extend Sunday's route north along the Rambla to Real de San Carlos for a full 20-miler. The terrain is flat-to-rolling — great for effort building at sea level before any altitude race prep.

Tonight (Saturday) and tomorrow after the run

You arrive July 11, a Saturday. Tomorrow is Sunday — which means the Feria de San Telmo, the city's best outdoor run, and a slow late-afternoon Palermo wander. Start easy tonight.

Palermo · 5–7 min walk

Palermo · 8–10 min

Your marquee dinner for the stay — book this the moment you read this. Trescha for the intimate 10-seat Michelin experience (15 courses, ~$200 pp). Don Julio for the great parrilla night if you want energy and wine. Both require 10+ days lead time from a Buenos Aires number.

Try the WhatsApp booking link for Don Julio. For Trescha, email directly via their site.

Tomorrow (Sunday, July 12) — after your morning run

Morning — Run in Bosques de Palermo An easy 8–12K through Parque Tres de Febrero — lakeside paths, the Rosedal circuit, cool winter air at sea level. Legs will feel great. Start at the corner of Av. del Libertador and Av. Sarmiento.
Recovery coffee LAB Coffee Roasters on Humboldt — the warm room, own-roasted beans, and excellent flat whites are the ideal post-run ritual. Pastry from Malcriada next door if you need fuel.

Late afternoon — Rooftop sunset Head back to Palermo and catch sunset from any of the neighbourhood rooftop bars before dinner. The winter light here at 6pm is extraordinary — that's the South Atlantic pulling the colours.
06 · Luxury Shopping

Alvear Avenue & beyond

Avenida Alvear in Recoleta is the most prestigious shopping street in South America — five blocks of French-palace facades, Hermès, Louis Vuitton, Cartier, and emerging Argentine houses. Palermo Soho is where the interesting local designers live.

Premier street

Avenida Alvear

Recoleta · 15 min Uber

Five blocks of French Belle Époque palaces and the city's most prestigious houses — Hermès (corner of Ayacucho), Louis Vuitton, Cartier, Ralph Lauren. New arrivals include Dolce & Gabbana and Gucci. The visual experience alone is worth the ride.

Opened 2026: a new open-air luxury mall in Recoleta with panoramic gastronomic terraces and art spaces. Golden Goose, Vilbrequin and international luxury confirmed. The city's most talked-about new retail destination — pair with an Alvear walk.

The city's best curated wine boutique — small-production Malbec, Torrontés, and natural wines from Mendoza, Salta, and Patagonia. The sommelier team knows exactly what's drinking well. Perfect for shipping a case home.
