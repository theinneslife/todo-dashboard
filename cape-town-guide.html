Orientation

What to know before you move

The essentials: money, the rate, how people actually talk, and the honest safety picture. Cape Town rewards confidence and clear-eyed awareness — especially by neighbourhood. Read the safety brief fully; the city is extraordinary but not uniform.

Currency

South African Rand

ZAR · symbol R. Prices written as R250 or 250,00. Easy mental maths: divide by 18 for a USD equivalent.

Exchange Rate

≈ R18

1 USD ≈ R17–R18 (May 2026 range). Conservative planning rate: R18. A R1,500 tasting menu costs roughly $83 USD.

Climate in May

17–20°C

Early winter — crisp, clear days with spectacular light, some rain. Table Mountain summit: near-freezing with wind. Always carry a shell on the mountain.

Getting Around

Uber / Bolt

Both work and are the safe default. Never use metered taxis hailed from the street — scams are common. Uber is ubiquitous from De Waterkant.

Running Altitude

17 m

Sea level — breathing is easy. The challenge is vertical. Table Mountain adds 1,085 m of gain from the lower cable station. Plan your pace on ascent.

Emergency & Essentials

10111

Police emergency. Tourist Safety Unit: 021 418 2852. Keep your host's number saved. ATMs: use those inside shopping malls or banks. Tap water in De Waterkant is safe to drink.

Talk like a local — Cape Town slang

Howzit — Standard greeting. "How are you?" Answer: "Lekker, thanks."
Lekker — Great, delicious, excellent. Universal positive.
Braai — BBQ. A sacred institution. Never call it a "barbecue."
Sharp / Sharp sharp — OK, understood, agreed. Extremely common.
Bru / Boet — Mate, buddy, brother. Casual address.
Lank — Very / a lot. "Lank good" = really good.
Now now — Soon (but ambiguous — could be 5 min or 2 hours).
Just now — Later (even more ambiguous than "now now").
Eish — Expression of surprise, frustration, or sympathy.
Dop — A drink. "One dop" = one drink. Also means to fail.
Ja well no fine — Resigned acceptance of a situation.
Smaak — To like or fancy. "I smaak it" = I love it.

Safety by neighbourhood — read this fully

Cape Town is extraordinary and unequal. The tourist zones are genuinely safe with standard urban awareness. But the city has hard no-go zones — know them and this trip is seamless.

  • V&A Waterfront — Highly secured, cameras everywhere, security on foot. One of the safest commercial zones in South Africa. Day or night, no issues.
  • De Waterkant / Green Point — Your home zone. Well-lit, walkable at night, restaurant-dense. Standard city awareness at 2am, otherwise relaxed.
  • Sea Point / Camps Bay / Clifton — Atlantic Seaboard suburbs. Very safe, popular, walkable promenades. Watch phones on the Sea Point beachfront at night.
  • Gardens / Kloof Street / Tamboerskloof — Busy, tourist-facing, fine at night in lit areas. Don't cut through dark alleys.
  • Bo-Kaap — Colourful Muslim quarter above the CBD. Fine during the day; Uber in and out, don't wander at night.
  • City Bowl CBD after dark — Desolate streets away from Long/Bree. Use Uber after dinner, don't walk unfamiliar blocks at night.
  • Woodstock — Old Biscuit Mill immediate area (Albert Road) is fine, especially Saturday market. Don't walk three blocks in any other direction.
  • Observatory — Has a rough edge. Lower Main Road restaurant strip is OK; avoid wandering solo.
  • Cape Flats (Mitchells Plain, Nyanga, Gugulethu) — No tourist reason to be here. Genuine danger. This is not an area to explore casually.
  • Khayelitsha / Langa / Bellville — Township areas. Go only as part of a formal guided township tour with an established local operator (highly recommended as an educational experience — but never alone).
  • Use Uber / Bolt exclusively for all rides. Never accept a quote from a driver who approaches you. Keep bags in your lap or the front footwell.
  • Phone rule: never use your phone while walking in unfamiliar areas — snatch theft from pedestrians is common. Put it away before you leave a restaurant.

03 · The Afternoons

Ten days, ten things worth doing

Cape Town's geography is the experience — two oceans, one mountain, 30,000 hectares of national park within the city limits. A mix of serious trail runs, cultural afternoons, and one helicopter flight you'll never forget. May's light is spectacular.

Ultra Priority

Lion's Head Sunrise Circuit

Signal Hill Road · 10 min drive from De Waterkant

The Cape Town run — 5 km loop, 669 m summit, technical chains and ladders near the top. Set your alarm for 5:30am, drive up, run it in the dark, summit at first light. The 360° view of the city, ocean, and Table Mountain at sunrise is one of the great experiences on earth.

Trail shoes with grip essential. Allow 1.5–2.5 hr depending on pace and queuing at the ladders. Quiet in May.

Iconic

Table Mountain — Summit & Contour

Lower Cableway Station · 15 min drive

Book digital tickets online — skip the physical queue entirely. May means crisper air and fewer crowds; the mountain's fynbos turns spectacular gold in autumn light. Hike Platteklip Gorge up (2.5 km, 670 m gain), cable car down. Or run the Contour Path for a serious training loop.

Cable car closes in high wind — check the day-of. Always carry a windproof layer. Summit: 1,085 m, near-freezing at dawn.

Signature Day

Cape Peninsula Circuit

Chapman's Peak · Boulders · Cape Point

The classic full-day loop: south along the Atlantic via Chapman's Peak Drive (vertiginous cliff road — stop and walk the overlook), Boulders Beach penguin colony (swim among African penguins in turquoise granite coves), then Cape Point for the dramatic southernmost lighthouse cliffs. Private driver strongly recommended.

Allow a full day — 5–7 hours with stops. Best paired with Foodbarn in Noordhoek for lunch on the way.

Bucket list

Helicopter — Full Peninsula Circuit

V&A Waterfront departure · 10 min from home

Book via Cape Town Helicopters or Civair from the Waterfront helipad. The full circuit covers: V&A, Table Mountain, the Twelve Apostles range, Clifton, Camps Bay, Hout Bay, Noordhoek, Cape Point, False Bay — everything you'll drive in a day, in 40 minutes from the air. R4,500–R7,000/person. Non-negotiable if you can afford it.

Cultural

Bo-Kaap Walk & Spice Route

Bo-Kaap · 10 min drive

The colourful Cape Malay quarter above the CBD. Cobblestoned streets, pastel-painted houses, the Bo-Kaap Museum, and the oldest working mosque in South Africa. Go with a local guide for context — the neighbourhood's history (forced removals, gentrification, identity) is layered and worth understanding properly.

Day only. Hire an Uber there and back.

Saturday ritual

Neighbour Goods Market at Old Biscuit Mill

Albert Road, Woodstock · Saturdays 9am–3pm

The beating creative heart of Cape Town — food producers, local designers, artisans, the city's best flat white, and wine by 11am. Go early for the full experience. Pairs naturally with an afternoon at The Pot Luck Club or Salon next door.

Uber in and out. The Albert Road strip is fine — don't walk further.

Coastal run

Kalk Bay & False Bay Coast

False Bay · 35 min drive south

Run the False Bay coastal path from St James to Kalk Bay (5–8 km). The Victorian tidal pool at St James, the fishing harbour at Kalk Bay, colourful antique shops and galleries on Main Road. Finish at Olympia Café for coffee and grilled fish. A completely different Cape Town from the Atlantic Seaboard.

Golden hour

Signal Hill Sunset

Signal Hill Road · 15 min drive

Free, easy, no hiking required. Drive to the Signal Hill parking area for 360° views of the city, Lion's Head, Table Mountain, and the Atlantic turning gold. The 6pm noon gun fires here daily. Cape Town's best-kept free sunset spot — and the one you'll send Brooke photos from every time you go up.

04 · The Weekend

Stellenbosch, Franschhoek & your long run in the Winelands

An hour east of Cape Town, the Cape Winelands deliver world-ranked estates, fine dining, and trail running in the Jonkershoek valley that will make you wish you trained here every week. May is the start of harvest's end — the vines are copper and gold, the tasting rooms quiet, and the trails in perfect condition for a 20+ mile long run.

Rent a car or hire a driver

The N2/N1 to Stellenbosch is a straightforward 45-minute drive — rent a car for independence. If you're tasting on Saturday, hire a private driver (R1,500–R2,500 round-trip from Cape Town with waiting time) and drink freely. The Jonkershoek long run is better as a self-drive early Sunday before the wine starts.

The shape of the Winelands weekend

Saturday morning — Stellenbosch Drive to Stellenbosch (45 min). Kanonkop Wine Estate for a serious Pinotage tasting — the closest thing South Africa has to Premier Cru. Then Waterford Estate's Wine & Chocolate tasting, one of the Cape's signature experiences. Walk the oak-lined streets of Stellenbosch town in the afternoon (Cape Dutch architecture, art galleries on Dorp Street).
Saturday dinner Rust en Vrede (see restaurants, #19) for the full estate fine-dining experience, or Dusk (43 Plein Street, Stellenbosch) — a 13-course zero-waste immersive menu using indigenous foraged ingredients. Remarkable. Book ahead. Overnight at Babylonstoren or Leeu Estates if you want the full Winelands experience.
Sunday — The Long Run: Jonkershoek Start at 7am from the Jonkershoek Nature Reserve entrance (R310 entrance fee, signed from Stellenbosch). The Twin Peaks trail is the best ultra-training terrain in the Winelands — 14–22 km depending on route, ~500 m gain, world-class scenery through indigenous Afromontane forest and fynbos. The valley runs ~200 m lower than Cape Town — you'll breathe easier. Carry 2L water and trail food. This is remote: no phone signal for much of the route. Leave route details with Brooke.
Sunday post-run Drive to Franschhoek (25 min from Stellenbosch). Babylonstoren garden tour and lunch at Babel restaurant — one of the most beautiful farm-to-table experiences in the world. Or La Petite Colombe (#20 on the restaurant list) for the definitive Winelands tasting menu. Drive home to Cape Town (1 hr).
Estate

Babylonstoren

Babylonstoren Road, Franschhoek

The most photographed estate in South Africa. Eight-hectare garden, farm-to-table restaurant (Babel), exceptional wine, and a hotel if you want to extend. The garden tour alone is worth the visit. Book Babel lunch in advance.

Run Terrain

Jonkershoek Nature Reserve

Jonkershoek Valley, Stellenbosch

Used in UTCT training routes. Twin Peaks trail (14–22 km, 500 m gain), Panorama circuit, and the Swartboskloof route for a longer day. Indigenous forest, mountain stream crossings, spectacular autumn-burnt fynbos in May. Trail shoes and 2L water minimum.

No phone signal inside the reserve. Share your route before you go.

Dinner

Dusk, Stellenbosch

43 Plein Street, Stellenbosch

13-course zero-waste tasting menu using indigenous foraged and fermented ingredients. Chefs Badenhorst and Austin. Immersive low-lit experience — one of the more adventurous meals in the whole Cape. Book well ahead: +27 21 023 4100.

Tasting

Waterford Wine & Chocolate

Blaauwklippen Road, Stellenbosch

Signature Waterford experience: eight wines paired with eight handcrafted dark chocolates, each designed to complement a specific varietal. One of the Cape's most refined and memorable estate experiences. Book online.

05 · Luxury Shopping

The Waterfront & beyond

Cape Town's luxury shopping anchors on the V&A Waterfront, with a strong independent and African-design scene in De Waterkant and Woodstock. The Watershed is the single best place to find genuinely South African-made objects — from ceramics to leather to jewellery.

Flagship

V&A Waterfront — Victoria Wharf

Dock Road, Green Point · 5 min from De Waterkant

Cape Town's premier luxury shopping destination — 450+ stores. Gucci, Burberry, Louis Vuitton, Dior are represented. A 2025/26 luxury wing expansion has added additional international fashion houses. A new luxury wing opened in phases between November 2025 and Easter 2026 — check what's new.

Best local design

The Watershed

V&A Waterfront · Dock Road

150+ local artisans under one roof — ceramics, fashion, jewellery, leather goods, accessories. The single best place to buy something authentically South African. Carrol Boyes (distinctive handmade functional art), Pichulik (ethical jewellery inspired by African design), AAFRICAA (curated contemporary design and basketware).

Everything here is made in South Africa. Worth at least an hour.

Boutique

Cape Quarter Lifestyle Village

De Waterkant · walking distance from home

Boutique, design-forward, far less crowded than the Waterfront. Scalini (luxury European clothing), independent jewellers, concept lifestyle stores. The local upscale alternative when you don't want the tourist energy of the Waterfront.

Creative district

Bree Street — Design & Gallery Mile

Bree Street · CBD

Cape Town's coolest street — independent fashion designers, art galleries, concept stores, wine bars. Walk the full length on a weekday afternoon. The Merchant by Merchant Magazine and various emerging South African fashion houses along the strip.

SA Design

Key South African Names

Various · V&A, Waterfront, Bree Street

Labels worth seeking: Okapi (SA leather goods, clean design), Carrol Boyes (sculptural homeware and cutlery — iconic gift), Poetry (elegant local women's clothing), The Coppery (Cape Winelands leather). The pieces nobody at home will have.

Saturday market

Neighbour Goods Market

Old Biscuit Mill, Woodstock · Saturdays

Not traditional retail but the city's creative production engine. Local designers, ceramicists, independent food producers, bespoke jewellery. If something unique is what you're after, start here Saturday morning before the restaurants open.

07 · The Pantry

Premium groceries & markets

For cooking-for-two nights at home, Cape Town's grocery scene is excellent — Woolworths Food is the Marks & Spencer Food equivalent, and Checkers FreshX is aggressively expanding its premium format. The Oranjezicht City Farm Market on Saturday mornings is the best farmers' market in the city.

Daily driver

Woolworths Food

V&A Waterfront · Camps Bay · multiple

South Africa's premium grocery standard. Think Marks & Spencer Food: exceptional fresh produce, outstanding ready-meals and deli counter (rotisserie chicken, pies, prepared salads), brilliant baked goods, fine wine section. The V&A Waterfront and Camps Bay Strip locations are closest. For everything you'd buy at Whole Foods, this is it.

Open 7 days. V&A location has the widest range; Camps Bay is more convenient from De Waterkant.

Oranjezicht City Farm Market

Granger Bay · V&A precinct · Saturdays 8am–2pm

The best farmers' market in Cape Town — certified organic produce, artisan bakers, small-batch cheese makers, heritage varietal veg, and some of the city's best prepared food stalls (biltong, Cape Malay curry, sourdough). Five minutes from De Waterkant. The Saturday morning ritual: go early, buy everything, cook that night.

Also runs a smaller Wednesday market. Peak season is May–September.

Neighbour Goods Market Food

Old Biscuit Mill, Woodstock · Saturdays 9am–3pm

Beyond the design and craft stalls, the Neighbour Goods Market has strong food producers — artisan charcuterie, specialty hot sauce, preserves, small-batch honey, fresh pasta. Combine with the market experience for a productive Saturday morning shop.

Pair with a Woodstock Bakery stop on the way in or out.
