Orientation

What to know before you move

Joburg rewards the prepared traveller and punishes the careless one. The money, the rate, how people talk, and — critically — how to navigate safely. Read the safety card fully before you step out.

Currency

ZAR · symbol R. Prices written R450 = 450 rand. Notes: R10, R20, R50, R100, R200.

Exchange Rate

≈ R17

1 USD ≈ R17 (April/May 2026 estimate). Divide by 17 for a quick USD check. R500 ≈ $29. R1,000 ≈ $59.

Altitude

1,753 m

≈ 5,751 ft — Highveld plateau. Expect noticeably harder breathing on your first 1–2 runs. Not your fitness. Hydrate aggressively. April/May is autumn: highs 20–24°C, lows 8–12°C, dry, sunny.

Getting Around

Uber / Bolt

Both work in Sandton/Rosebank and are the safe default. Gautrain runs O.R. Tambo → Sandton in 15 min — use it from the airport. No street taxis. No minibus taxis.

Talk like a local — South African slang

Howzit — How are you? (standard greeting)
Lekker — Nice, great, delicious, cool
Eish — Expression of surprise, dismay, or emphasis
Sharp — OK, understood, got it, cool
Just now — In a while (NOT immediately — could be 30 min)
Now now — Sooner than just now, but still not right now
Jol — A party or a good time; "let's jol" = let's go out
Braai — BBQ; sacred South African institution
Bru / Boet — Mate, brother (bru is more Cape; boet is Joburg)
Sies — Gross, disgusting (pronounced "sis")
Shame — Sympathy or cuteness ("Ag shame, that's adorable")
Robot — Traffic light. "Turn left at the robot."

⚠ Safety — read this fully. Joburg is not like other cities.

  • Sandton and Rosebank are legitimately safe during the day — private security everywhere, busy streets, mall entrances with guards. Walk these zones freely in daylight.
  • No walking after dark anywhere — not even in Sandton. Uber door-to-door, every time, no exceptions. This is non-negotiable in Joburg.
  • The CBD, Hillbrow, Berea, and Joubert Park are no-go zones for tourists, day or night. Do not go, even as a curiosity drive.
  • Uber only — never hail a street cab, never take a minibus taxi (minibus taxis are high-risk for crime). Bolt is also safe.
  • Smash-and-grab is real — keep bags on your lap, not on seats. Lock car doors as soon as you get in. Don't leave anything visible on seats at red lights.
  • No phones out on the street — not to navigate, not to photograph. Step inside a doorway or café. Same rule for cameras and visible watches.
  • ATMs: Sandton City mall ATMs only — inside the mall, during the day. Never use outdoor/street ATMs, even in Sandton.
  • Maboneng and Braamfontein are creative hubs with tours — go with a guide or join a tour group. They're improving but require awareness; solo wandering is not advised.
  • Soweto tours: guided only — with a reputable operator. The township is safe on a guided tour and genuinely extraordinary; just don't self-drive it solo.
  • Private transfers from O.R. Tambo for airport runs if not taking Gautrain. Pre-book your hotel's driver or a verified service — don't use unmarked "taxi" offers at arrivals.
  • Emergency: 10111 (police) · 10177 (ambulance) · 112 (mobile emergency)
  • The short version: Sandton/Rosebank by day = fine. Anywhere by night = Uber. Act confident, stay alert, don't flash valuables. Thousands of visitors come and go without incident every week.

03 · The Afternoons

Three days, six moves

Joburg asks you to engage with its contradictions — the astonishing, world-class Apartheid Museum alongside the gleaming towers of Sandton; Soweto's Vilakazi Street alongside the Maboneng arts scene. The city rewards curiosity and punishes tourist-bubble isolation.

Essential · half day

Apartheid Museum

Gold Reef City area · 30 min Uber from Sandton

Among the most powerful museums anywhere in the world — a meticulously designed journey through the history and dismantling of apartheid. Allow 3 hours minimum; you'll want more. The entrance experience (being assigned 'White' or 'Non-White' at random) is unforgettable and intentional.

Go on your first full day, before anything else. It contextualises the entire city.

Guided · half day

Soweto Township Tour

Soweto · 20 min drive from Sandton

Guided tour of the world's most famous township — Vilakazi Street (the only street to have housed two Nobel Laureates: Mandela and Tutu), the Hector Pieterson Memorial, the Orlando Towers. Not a poverty tour — a living, vibrant city of 1.3 million people. Profound and energising in equal measure.

Book with Urban Buds or similar reputable operator. Guided only — do not self-drive.

Culture

Constitution Hill

Braamfontein · 20 min from Sandton

South Africa's Constitutional Court sits on the site of a notorious old prison — Number Four — where Gandhi, Mandela, and thousands of others were held. The architecture of the court incorporates prison bricks, prisoners' handprints, and stories. Guided tour is essential.

Arts + market

Maboneng Precinct + Market on Main

Maboneng · 25 min from Sandton

"Place of light" in Sotho — Joburg's most creative precinct, a warehouse district turned arts hub with galleries, studios, street murals, boutiques, and Joburg's best Sunday market (Market on Main, every Sunday 10am–3pm). Home of the Bean and Firebird Coffee are both here.

Market on Main runs Sundays. May 1 (Workers' Day) may have a special market — confirm ahead.

Unexpected

Gold Reef City + Old Mine Tour

South of Sandton · 30 min

Joburg was literally built on gold — descend into a real 19th-century working gold mine shaft (220m underground) to understand why this city exists. Adjacent to the Apartheid Museum, making it an easy half-day double.

Signature Joburg

Neighbourgoods Market

Braamfontein · Saturdays 9am–2pm

Joburg's best Saturday morning market — artisan food, excellent coffee from Rosetta Roastery on-site, local makers, craft beer, and a crowd of the city's most interesting people. Runs Saturday 9am–2pm. May 2 is a Saturday — your perfect final morning before heading to Singita.

Go by Uber; Braamfontein is fine on market day with the crowd present.

04 · The Side-Trip

Pilanesberg — Big Five, malaria-free, 2.5 hours

The cleanest Big Five safari from Joburg: 140km northwest in the North West Province, built inside the crater of an ancient volcano, malaria-free, home to 7,000 animals including lion, elephant, rhino, leopard, and buffalo. May 1 is Workers' Day — a public holiday in SA, with the reserve fully operational. Build this as a May 1 day trip.

Drive or hire a guide?

Self-drive in Pilanesberg is genuinely viable — 2WD roads throughout, well-marked game loops, and the intimacy of stopping when you want. Hire a high-clearance rental from OR Tambo (Avis or Budget, booked ahead). Alternatively, hire a driver-guide from Sandton — they'll know the best game loops and sighting hotspots. The guided open-jeep safari from Joburg (via Viator/GetYourGuide) is the easiest option for a single day. Leave Sandton by 4:30–5:00am to hit the gate at opening (6:30am in autumn) for the best game movement.

May 1 · Workers' Day · The day trip shape

4:00am wake — long run in Joburg first Do your long run before the drive. Emmarentia Dam loop (Joburg's best urban running venue) or the Braamfontein Spruit trail: a 15–20km continuous green corridor from Emmarentia through Delta Park toward Parkhurst. Run with one other person minimum; 5–6am is safe and well-trafficked by Joburg runners. Altitude note: at 1,753m your pace will feel ~45 sec/mile harder than sea level. That's the air, not your fitness — don't chase the watch. Conditions: autumn dawn, 8–12°C at start, dry. Carry water; the Spruit has no fountains.
Recover + drive north Quick shower, load the car — Pilanesberg is 2 hrs 15 min on the N14/R565. Arrive at Manyane or Bakubung gate for the mid-morning game circuit (still good movement until ~10:30am). Elephant and rhino are frequently seen in the Mankwe Dam area.
Game drive 9:30am–1:00pm Best loop: Manyane Gate → Mankwe Dam hide → Lengau Loop → Ruighoek Loop → Buffalo Thorn Loop. The Mankwe Dam hide is a fixed hide over water — lion, rhino, and elephant all drink here. April/May is excellent: vegetation thinning after summer rain, making animals far easier to spot than January/February.
Lunch at Bakubung or Tshukudu Bakubung Bush Lodge has a solid lunch restaurant overlook the waterhole (elephants visible from your table). Tshukudu Game Lodge is more intimate and upmarket. Book a table when you reserve the day trip.
Afternoon circuit + drive back Optional second game circuit 3–5:30pm (sunset movement is good), then the 2.5-hr drive back to Sandton. Dinner reservation at Marble or Nobu when you're back — you'll have earned it.

Run safety inside Pilanesberg: Do not run inside the reserve — Big Five wildlife means running is genuinely dangerous. All running should be done in Joburg before the drive. If you want trail running near the park, Sun City Resort (adjacent) has running paths and a maintained road loop through the resort grounds.

Alt for a full overnight: Bakubung Bush Lodge or Tshukudu Game Lodge both do overnights — a May 1 overnight + early May 2 game drive before heading south toward Singita would be spectacular. You'd miss the Neighbourgoods Market but gain a dawn drive.

06 · Luxury Shopping

Diamond Walk & beyond

Sandton City and the adjacent Nelson Mandela Square form one of Africa's largest and most prestigious retail complexes. The Diamond Walk inside Sandton City is your European luxury-brand corridor. Rosebank Mall and Melrose Arch round out the circuit.

Sandton · walkable from Sandton hotels

Africa's most prestigious shopping destination. The Diamond Walk corridor houses Prada, Gucci, Louis Vuitton, Dolce & Gabbana, Alexander McQueen, Giorgio Armani, Jimmy Choo, and Cartier. A newly opened Rolex Boutique by Shemer is the star addition for 2025/26. Security-managed, immaculate.

Pedestrian-friendly mixed-use precinct — European in feel, with upscale boutiques, design studios, galleries, and Cecconi's restaurant. The most pleasant shopping streetscape in Joburg; easy to wander without being inside a mall. Premium lifestyle brands and local independents.

Running — routes & altitude notes
Best trail — your go-to

Braamfontein Spruit

Emmarentia → Parkhurst → Bryanston · 15–20km linear

Joburg's finest urban running corridor — a 15–20km continuous trail following the Spruit river through Emmarentia Dam, Delta Park, Parkhurst, and Bryanston. Green, shaded (autumn foliage), largely traffic-free. Start at Emmarentia Dam. Run with one other person; 5:30–7:30am is the safest window when the trail is most populated by locals.

Groups of 2+ strongly recommended. Mornings only.

Altitude training

Emmarentia Dam Loop

Emmarentia · ~5km lake loop

The Joburg runner's morning ritual — a scenic lake loop through Emmarentia Dam and Botanical Gardens. Parkrun happens here Saturday mornings (7:30am, free entry — May 2 is a Saturday). The altitude at 1,753m means a 8:00/mi effort feels like 7:30 back home. Front-load hydration the night before; the Highveld air is bone dry in autumn.

The Fresh Earth Food Store

Greenside / Northcliff · 20 min from Sandton

Joburg's dedicated organic and health food store — local farm produce, cold-pressed juices, organic protein, supplements, and a deli section. The move if you're looking for clean fuel for a high-mileage training block. Confirm current hours ahead of visit.

Saturday market

Neighbourgoods Market

Braamfontein · Saturdays 9am–2pm

The best fresh market experience in Joburg — artisan produce, local honey, biltong suppliers, fresh bread, cold-pressed juices, and excellent street food. A social experience as much as a grocery run. Go by Uber on May 2 before departing for Singita.

Safari provisions

Biltong & road food at any Woolworths

Sandton City / Rosebank Mall

Before Singita: stock a small cooler bag from Woolworths Food. Biltong (SA's air-dried beef — better than jerky), droëwors (dried sausage), Woolworths premium nuts, and a bottle of Stellenbosch Chenin Blanc. The provender of the Highveld road.
