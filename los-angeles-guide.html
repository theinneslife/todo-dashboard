Orientation

What to know before you move

LA is a city of parallel universes — WeHo's restaurant row, Silver Lake's coffee and record stores, Venice's barefoot-billionaire energy. Getting around is either a freeway roulette or a Lyft; running is the only sensible traffic bypass. April is the sweet spot before the heat and before tourist season peaks.

Currency

US Dollar

USD · No conversion needed. Tipping 18–22% is standard at restaurants. Many LA coffee spots are cash-friendly but card is universal.

Getting Around

Lyft / Drive

Lyft and Uber both work. For WeHo–Silver Lake–Venice, Lyft is the default. A rental car opens Malibu, Joshua Tree, and Pacific Coast Highway — get one for the weekend.

Climate — April

68°F avg

Low humidity, Pacific breezes, daytime highs 68–74°F, mornings around 54°F. Layers for early runs. May gray (marine layer) can roll in at dawn — burns off by 10am.

Neighborhoods

WeHo + SilLake

West Hollywood for dining, nightlife, Equinox, Whole Foods. Silver Lake for coffee, running Griffith, record shops, and the best casual restaurant scene in the city.

Driving Culture

The 405

Use freeway numbers with "the" — the 405, the 101, the 10. Rush hour is 7–9am and 4–7pm and genuinely painful. Run or Lyft at those times, drive everything else.

Parking

Read signs

Street sweeping signs are enforced ruthlessly. Apps: ParkMobile for meters, SpotHero for garages. The Grove, Abbot Kinney, and Melrose all have paid lots — budget 15 min to find a spot.

Staying smart — safety in LA

  • WeHo and Silver Lake are safe, walkable neighborhoods by day and after dark.
  • Never leave anything visible in a parked car. Smash-and-grab is the most common crime — empty the car every time.
  • Runyon Canyon, Griffith Park, and The Strand are safe for solo dawn runs; bring a phone and ID.
  • Some blocks near DTLA and Skid Row have significant homeless encampments — walk with normal urban awareness.
  • Venice Boardwalk is lively and mostly fine; the alleys behind it deserve less attention at night.
  • Wildfire smoke is rare in April but check AQI before long trail runs in canyon areas.
  • Tap water is safe. Ocean swimming: check surfrider.org for beach water quality before going in.
  • Emergency: 911. Non-emergency LAPD: 877-ASK-LAPD.

03 · The Afternoons

Six afternoons that define the city

LA resists the "attractions" frame — it's best experienced through neighborhoods and light. These six progressively unfold the city from beauty through culture through coast, with one genuinely non-obvious LA experience in the mix.

Signature

Getty Center

Brentwood · Free admission, timed reservation required

Richard Meier's hilltop complex houses one of the world's great Impressionist collections, but April is really about the Central Garden — azalea maze in full bloom, seasonal plantings at peak. Stay through golden hour when the travertine stone turns amber. Book the timed entry in advance; stay the whole afternoon and have lunch at the café.

The Central Garden in April is as beautiful as any garden in California. Don't rush through.

Athletic + iconic

Griffith Observatory at dusk

Los Feliz · Hike up or drive

Hike the 3-mile trail from the Greek Theatre parking lot (adds a real climb to your day) or drive up and walk the observatory terrace. The moment when the city transitions from natural to electric light — golden hour collapsing into the grid of LA — is the most affecting view in the city. Free. Go at 7pm.

Running credit: the hike up from Los Feliz is genuinely hard — 600ft gain in 1.5 miles.

Street-level

Abbot Kinney Boulevard

Venice · Walk the whole mile

The best single street in LA for wandering — a mile of independent design, coffee, fashion, bookshops, and some of the city's best restaurants. Start at Gjusta for breakfast or Gjelina for dinner. Midday is ideal for browsing; the street fills up by late afternoon. Pair with the beachfront path for a full Venice day.

The drive

PCH to Malibu + Getty Villa

Pacific Coast Highway · Rent a convertible

Rent a convertible for this one — the Pacific on your left, Santa Monica Mountains on your right, open road. Stop at El Matador Beach (dramatic sea stacks, worth a 30-min scramble), continue to the Getty Villa (a reconstructed Roman country house full of extraordinary antiquities; free, book ahead), then Neptune's Net for grilled fish at a beach shack that's been there forever.

Getty Villa requires a timed reservation — book before you go.

Contemporary art

The Broad + Grand Central Market

DTLA · Free timed entry

Jeff Koons, Basquiat, Kara Walker, and Cindy Sherman in one building — free with a timed-entry reservation. Walk two blocks to Grand Central Market for the best cheap eating in the city, including the famous Holbox tostadas. Cap the afternoon at Bar Ama or Otium for dinner.

Genuinely LA

Runyon Canyon at golden hour

Hollywood Hills · Free

Two miles of moderately hard trail rising above Hollywood, open at dawn and until sunset. The view from the ridge catches the entire basin from the ocean to downtown. More LA energy than any tourist attraction — equal parts serious hike and social scene. Sunset is the move. Bring water.

Can combine with a Fairfax dinner afterward — Jon & Vinny's, Republique, or Salt's Cure are all close.

04 · The Weekend

Joshua Tree & your 16-mile desert run

April is the sweet spot for Joshua Tree — wildflowers potentially still showing, morning lows in the low 50s for running, afternoon highs in the mid-70s before summer heat sets in. Two hours forty-five minutes from WeHo on the I-10 East. This is the stronger all-around weekend over Santa Barbara for a long run: dramatic terrain, no trails like it in the world.

Drive it yourself — it's a straight shot

Unlike mountain roads elsewhere, the I-10 East to Joshua Tree is as simple as California driving gets — two hours of freeway, then 45 minutes of park road. Leave WeHo by 7am Saturday to beat desert heat. A rental car with a cooler loaded before you leave is all the crew logistics you need for the long run. No Pico y Placa, no mountain hairpins.

The shape of the weekend — April 26–27

Saturday AM — Drive out Leave WeHo 7am. I-10 East to Hwy 62. Stop at Chiriaco Summit café (historic WWII-era diner, oddly great pie) if you need a break. Enter the park via the West Entrance at Joshua Tree village. Hit Barker Dam trailhead to walk the short loop and get a feel for the terrain before Sunday's run.
Saturday PM — Pioneertown Drive 10 minutes north to Pioneertown — a preserved 1940s movie set that became a real town. Pappy & Harriet's Pioneertown Palace is a legendary roadhouse: live music most nights, mesquite-grilled ribs, cold beer under the desert stars. This is the Saturday night of the trip. Book a table ahead.
Sunday 6am — The 16-18 miler Start the Boy Scout Trail from the Quail Springs trailhead (park Hwy, 5 miles east of the West Entrance). The trail runs north 8 miles to the park boundary through surreal boulder fields and Joshua tree groves — turn around at mile 8 for your 16-mile. Sandy, rocky terrain; no shade; morning lows 48–55°F at 6am in April rising to ~72°F by 10am. Start no later than 6:30am to finish before heat. Carry minimum 2L water — no water sources on trail. No crew access mid-route; leave a cooler at the car for your finish.
Sunday PM — Drive back Post-run recovery: Crossroads Café in Joshua Tree village for scrambled eggs and locally roasted coffee. Slow drive back along Hwy 62 to I-10, back in WeHo by 4–5pm. Legs earned a Nobu reservation this week.

Santa Barbara alternative: 90 minutes north on the 101. Flat beach run along Cabrillo Blvd (ocean-front, 10-mile out-and-back along the coast), wine country in the Funk Zone, and a much more relaxed Saturday night at The Lark or Bouchon. Choose this if you want zero logistics and a 16-miler on pavement with ocean views.

05 · Right Now

Tonight (Thursday) and tomorrow after the run

You land April 24 — Thursday. Keep tonight easy: you've traveled, you want to feel the city without fighting it. Tomorrow Friday is when you eat seriously. Book n/naka or Kato for Friday night before you read the rest of this.

Tonight · easy

Gjusta or Gjelina

Venice · 15–20 min from WeHo

Gjusta for a late, unpretentious plate from the case — smoked fish, a grain bowl, a pastry. If you want a proper table, Gjelina next door is the wood-fired Venice classic. Either is the right energy for a Thursday arrival: excellent food, low-key atmosphere, no booking drama.

Walk Abbot Kinney after dinner — the street at night is a different LA than the day.

Tomorrow night · the marquee

n/naka or Kato

Palms / DTLA · book now

n/naka is the hardest reservation in LA — Niki Nakayama's 13-course kaiseki is worth weeks of planning. If n/naka is full, Kato (two Michelin stars, 2026) is the other answer: Jonathan Yao's Taiwanese omakase is arguably the more exciting room right now. Either way, book the moment you land and be prepared for a 2–3 hour dinner.

n/naka books weeks out. Kato: reserve via Tock. Both are worth whatever hoops you jump through.

Friday, after morning coffee and a run

Morning — Griffith Park or Runyon Griffith Park's trails from the Fern Dell entrance give you 5–10 miles of shaded canyon running with no cars. Runyon Canyon from the Hollywood entrance is harder (600ft) and shorter but rewards with the ridge view. Both are 15 minutes from WeHo.
Recovery coffee Go Get Em Tiger on Larchmont — walkable from Griffith, the Kyoto latte is the post-run ritual. Or Intelligentsia on Sunset if you're coming from Silver Lake direction. Both are exceptional and never feel like a tourist stop.
Afternoon — The Getty or The Broad The Getty Center for a slow afternoon in the gardens (reserve before you go; it's free). Or The Broad in DTLA for 2 hours of contemporary art followed by Grand Central Market for the best cheap eat in LA at Holbox's counter.
Evening — the Michelin reservation n/naka or Kato. Budget 2.5–3 hours. These are the kinds of meals you still talk about two years later. Don't schedule anything after.
06 · Luxury Shopping

Rodeo Drive, Melrose & beyond

Beverly Hills has the flagship houses; Melrose has the serious concept stores; Abbot Kinney has the independent luxury; and Fairfax has the pieces you can't find anywhere else. LA's shopping geography is as spread as the city itself — plan by neighborhood, not by a single mall.

The Strip

Rodeo Drive

Beverly Hills · 2-block stretch

More than 100 luxury brands on two blocks — Chanel, Dior, Hermès (newly expanded to 25,000 sq ft), Cartier, Valentino. The Louis Vuitton Frank Gehry-designed flagship is in development and worth checking. The Rodeo Collection gallery mall is quieter for serious browsing. Come late morning on a weekday to avoid the tour bus crowd.

Concept store

Maxfield

Melrose Ave · the real one

LA's most serious independent concept boutique — Rick Owens, Comme des Garçons, Maison Margiela, Raf Simons, rarities you won't find at department stores. The building is an event in itself. This is where serious LA fashion people shop; not a tourist destination, a destination.

Curated luxury

The Webster LA

West Hollywood

The Miami luxury concept store's LA outpost — tightly curated selection of fashion, art, and accessories across multiple designers. Beautiful space, excellent edit. The WeHo location pulls from a different sensibility than the Rodeo Drive flagships.

Gucci Salon

Gucci Salon Melrose

Melrose Ave · appointment preferred

Gucci's high-concept exclusive salon on Melrose — a different experience from the Rodeo Drive flagship. Custom pieces, private shopping, a genuinely elevated level of service. Best experienced as an appointment rather than a drop-in.

LA institution

Fred Segal

West Hollywood

The WeHo institution that invented the LA boutique concept — curated fashion, accessories, beauty, and a rotating cast of emerging designers. The energy is effortlessly LA in the best sense: polished but not precious.

Local luxury basics

James Perse

Multiple · Melrose, Malibu

LA-born luxury basics — the cashmere tees, the travel-weight shirts, the shorts that work from morning run to dinner. Perfect for topping up a travel wardrobe with things you'll wear for years. The Melrose flagship is the full experience.

Independent, Venice

Abbot Kinney Boulevard

Venice · the whole mile

Local designers, concept stores, vintage, and galleries. Tortoise General Store for beautiful Japanese-made goods; Warby Parker got its start here; Heist for jewelry. The independent luxury LA version — nothing here is what you'd find at a mall.

The Grove

The Grove + Original Farmers Market

Fairfax District

LA's nicest outdoor mall adjoining the 1930s Original Farmers Market — Nordstrom, Apple, luxury beauty, and a dozen good food stalls in the adjacent market. If you need everything in one place, this is the move. The trolley running between them is a genuinely charming touch.

The LA standard for a premium gym experience — day passes available (~$35), full equipment, sauna, classes, and the city's best-equipped facilities. The WeHo location is the one. If you're at home in Equinox elsewhere, this is your gym.

The treadmill + floor intervals class that took over LA — high-energy 50-minute sessions with class packs available. The WeHo location is the original; lines for popular instructors form. Excellent cross-training between long run days.

Running — free

Griffith Park Trails

Los Feliz / Hollywood Hills

4,200 acres with 53 miles of trails and fire roads. The Mount Hollywood Loop (8.1 miles, 1,200ft gain) is your ultra training route inside the city. Fern Dell entrance for shaded canyon start. Open at dawn. Carry water — no aid stations on most routes.

Running — flat

The Strand (Marvin Braude Bike Trail)

Santa Monica → Redondo Beach

22 miles of flat, ocean-breeze-filled beachfront path — Santa Monica Pier south to Redondo Beach. Your zero-elevation alternative to Griffith when you need easy miles. Run at dawn before cyclists fill the path. The Pacific on your left the entire way.

08 · The Pantry

Erewhon and the premium grocery scene

LA invented the premium grocery store as lifestyle destination — Erewhon is the apex, but the city has a full ecosystem of farmers markets, organic grocers, and specialty shops that make cooking-for-two nights genuinely excellent. Erewhon is not optional; it is a Los Angeles experience in itself.

Erewhon: the store that's also an event

13 locations, all in greater LA. Think Whole Foods on steroids — niche organic brands, functional beverages, boutique supplements, the finest produce, and smoothie bars where $22 drinks are considered normal. The WeHo, Venice, and Silver Lake locations are the closest. Their in-house smoothie bar is genuinely excellent. Budget twice what you think.

The icon · 13 LA locations

Erewhon Market

Venice · West Hollywood · Silver Lake · Calabasas · citywide

The luxury organic experience — strict ingredient standards, boutique and small-batch brands, $20–30 smoothies that are legitimately good. Organic produce, specialty supplements, prepared foods that rival restaurant quality. The Venice and WeHo locations are closest. This is not a grocery run; it's an afternoon in itself.

The smoothie bar collaborations (celebrity partnerships) change seasonally — worth trying whatever's current.

Reliable · multiple

Whole Foods Market

WeHo (Santa Monica Blvd) · Silver Lake · Westwood · multiple

Wide selection, reliable quality, strong prepared foods section, good wine. The WeHo location on Santa Monica Blvd is close and well-stocked. Silver Lake has the local neighborhood feel. For when you want everything in one place without the Erewhon premium.

Wednesday + Saturday

Santa Monica Farmers Market

Arizona Ave · Santa Monica

One of the most important farmers markets in America — many of LA's best chefs (including Jeremy Fox of Rustic Canyon) shop here on Wednesday mornings. Extraordinary seasonal produce, local honey, cut flowers, prepared food vendors. Wednesday 8am–1pm is the premium market; Saturday has the larger crowd.

Wednesday 8–11am is when the chefs are there — the best window for produce selection.

Sunday market

Hollywood Farmers Market

Ivar Ave · Hollywood · Sundays 8am–1pm

The largest and busiest Sunday market in the city — 160+ vendors, exceptional variety, and the best prepared food selection of any LA market. Excellent for stocking up if your Sunday isn't the Joshua Tree weekend.

Sunday boutique

Melrose Place Farmers Market

Melrose Place · Sundays 10am–2pm

Smaller, curated, and chic — the farmers market that feels like a design event. Organic produce, artisan food, and the same aesthetic energy as the surrounding boutiques. Pairs perfectly with a Melrose shopping afternoon.

Regional upscale chain

Gelson's Markets

Fairfax · Los Feliz · Beverly Hills · multiple

The upscale regional alternative to Whole Foods — excellent butcher counter, great cheese selection, strong wine and spirits. The Fairfax location is convenient for WeHo; Los Feliz is perfect after Griffith. Feels like a neighborhood grocery done properly.

Veteran organic

Rainbow Acres Natural Foods

Mar Vista

A comprehensive selection of organic and natural groceries, supplements, and health products — a veteran of LA's natural food scene before it was fashionable. Knowledgeable staff, friendly atmosphere, and a local-institution quality that Erewhon's rapid expansion can't replicate.
