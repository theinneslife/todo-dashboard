Orientation

What to know before you move

Sea level, desert sun, and Sea of Cortez blue. April is genuinely the best month to be here — mild days, low humidity, whale sharks at the tail of their season. The essentials up front.

Currency

Mexican Peso

MXN · symbol $. USD accepted nearly everywhere along the Corridor — but you'll get better rates paying in pesos at local spots.

Exchange Rate

≈ 17.50

1 USD ≈ 17.30–17.70 MXN (June 2026 — likely similar in April). Divide pesos by 17.5 for a quick dollar figure.

April Climate

22–28°C

Days warm and dry, lows ~17–20°C. Desert UV is intense even at this temperature. Run before 7:30am. Humidity is still low — ideal. The one week before summer's heat arrives.

Getting Around

Uber / Hotel

Uber operates in both Cabo San Lucas and San José. Corridor resorts: pre-book hotel shuttles or private transfers — the 30km stretch between towns has no reliable street hail.

Running — April Notes

5:30–7:30am

Desert heat and UV hit hard by 9am even in April. Run early — Malecón San José at sunrise is flat, paved, quiet, and beautiful. Carry at least 20oz more water than you think you need. The dry Baja air dehydrates faster than anywhere in Latin America. No altitude adjustment needed: you're at sea level.

Best routes: Malecón / Paseo de los Pescadores (San José) · Estero de San José trail (soft, birdy) · Medano Beach at low tide (sand work) · Pedregal hills (Cabo San Lucas, elevation, technical)

Safety — Los Cabos tourist zone

  • Los Cabos is consistently ranked Mexico's safest tourist destination. The Marina, Medano Beach, Art District and Corridor are low-risk.
  • Use Uber after dark rather than hailing random taxis on the street. Hotel-arranged transport is always the safe default.
  • Don't leave bags or valuables on the beach unattended, even in broad daylight near busy stretches.
  • The marina area in Cabo San Lucas is busy and safe at night. Dark beach stretches away from the main strip: take a cab.
  • San José del Cabo centro and Art District are walkable and safe by day; Uber after a late dinner.
  • Avoid drinking tap water — use bottled or resort-filtered. Ice at major restaurants is fine.
  • Withdraw cash from ATMs inside La Comer, Costco, or bank branches — not outdoor street machines.
  • Emergency line: 911. Hospital Ángeles Los Cabos (San José) is the area's top private hospital.
01 · The Table

Twenty restaurants worth the ride

Los Cabos punches far above its size. Cliff-face dining rooms above the Pacific, a farm-to-table movement rooted in the Sierra de la Laguna foothills, and serious chefs who've set up serious tables in the Corridor and San José Art District. Book the top tier at least two weeks ahead.

World-class tier — book immediately
01
El Farallon
The apex of Baja dining. Carved into the sea cliffs at the Waldorf Astoria Pedregal, accessed through a candlelit tunnel cut through the mountain. Pacific seafood market-to-table, the most dramatic setting in Los Cabos. Two-week booking lead minimum.

Enrique Olvera (Pujol, Atla) helms this Baja-Pacific Rim seafood menu from a cliff-edge room above Land's End. The aguachile and raw bar are exceptional. One of the most serious culinary efforts in the entire corridor. Book it early.

Cocina de Autor — Grand Velas Los Cabos
The most technically ambitious kitchen in Los Cabos — avant-garde tasting format with rotating Michelin-starred visiting chefs. An ultra-luxury resort table that earns its price. Pairs well with a spa day at the same property.
Corridor

Jazamango
Chef Javier Plascencia's farm-driven room in San José's Art District. The definitive Baja California farm-to-table restaurant — produce-first, ingredient-obsessed, beautiful terrace. The most respected local-ingredients table in the region. Book ahead.

A dining scene that punches up

Los Cabos was once known only for resort buffets and fish tacos. Today it carries Michelin Guide recognition, a farm-to-table movement anchored in the Sierra de la Laguna, and Enrique Olvera himself running a cliff-top kitchen. The table has been worth making reservations for.

The Sea of Cortez & the far south

Jacques Cousteau called it the world's aquarium. In April, whale sharks still cruise the warm shallows near La Paz, humpbacks breach off Land's End, and Cabo Pulmo's living coral reef — a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve — holds more marine density than almost anywhere in the Pacific. Get underwater.

03 · The Afternoons

One experience a day

April is the sweet spot. Desert wildflowers are at peak bloom after winter rains, whale season is at its tail end, the water temperature is perfect, and the crowds are thinning from spring break. Here is a progression from easygoing to genuinely unforgettable.

Signature · day one

El Arco glass-bottom boat

Cabo San Lucas Marina · 30–45 min

The mandatory first afternoon. Glass-bottom boat around Land's End: El Arco arch from the water, Pelican Rock, Neptune's Finger sea lion colony, the exact point where the Pacific meets the Sea of Cortez. Book directly at the marina dock on arrival — tours run all morning and early afternoon. Choose a late morning departure for better light on the Arch.

The private charter version (sunset slot, champagne) is the upgrade worth making for one evening.

April special

Whale watching — tail of season

CSL Marina · 2–3 hr tour

Humpback whale season in Los Cabos runs December through April. Your trip falls right at the final days — book the first morning of your trip before season closes entirely. Operators with early-April availability: Cabo Adventures and Sunrider tours. Not guaranteed, but April humpbacks are still common. A gray or humpback breach 200 meters off your boat is a memory that stays.

Book day one of your trip — don't wait. Some operators close April 15; others run through end of April.

Best underwater

Cabo Pulmo snorkeling

~1.5–2 hr drive NE · East Cape

A UNESCO World Biosphere Reserve with the only living coral reef in the Sea of Cortez. Marine density here rivals the Galápagos — bull sharks, schooling fish walls, sea turtles, stingrays. Book a guided day trip from Los Cabos with gear and lunch included. Plan for a full day: the drive is worthwhile. April water temp is ~24°C — perfect.

Book via Cabo Trek or a reputable dive operator. The East Cape road is paved and manageable.

Desert & wildflowers

ATV / UTV desert tour

Corridor desert trailheads

Fire up a UTV and head through the Sierra arroyos and desert tracks to Migrino Beach. April is wildflower peak — the cardon cacti bloom and the desert floor turns color after winter rains. Most operators offer a 2–3 hour guided circuit with a Pacific beach stop at the end. Cabo Adventures and Desert Park Tours are the reputable options. Dusty but glorious.

Alternatively: a camel + ATV combo from the same outfitters — absurd and worth it.

Sunset ritual

Private sunset sailing cruise

CSL Marina · 2 hr

Catamaran at golden hour from the marina: champagne, appetizers, El Arco silhouetted against the sun going into the Pacific. Every Los Cabos visitor does this once — the difference between a shared group tour and a private charter is enormous. Book the private version for just the two of you. Magical April evening light.

Wild & quiet

Estero de San José kayaking

San José del Cabo · morning

The SJdC estuary is a protected freshwater lagoon where the river meets the sea — 350+ bird species, blue herons, pelicans, ospreys. A morning kayak here is silent, wild, and completely at odds with the resort strip 10 minutes away. Run the Malecón, shower, paddle the estuary. That's a morning.

Go with a guide for best routes and bird identification — several operators based at the estuary entrance.

Sport

Half-day sportfishing charter

CSL Marina · 4–5 hr AM

April is excellent for dorado (mahi-mahi) and yellowfin tuna in the Sea of Cortez. Book a private panga or a larger sportfisher from the marina — several charter operators rate well on TripAdvisor. Have them pack the catch on ice and drop it at your villa or arrange a cook-it-yourself dinner. Baja sportfishing is world-famous for good reason.

Best outfitters: Pisces Sportfishing, Gaviota Sportfishing. Book 48 hrs ahead minimum.

Art & culture

Thursday Art Walk — SJdC Gallery District

Art District · San José del Cabo

You arrive on April 17 — a Thursday. The Gallery District's famous Art Walk runs October through June, every Thursday 5–9pm. About 20 galleries open simultaneously, artists in residence, street food, live music. The premier cultural event in Baja California Sur, and you walk into it on night one. Have dinner at Jazamango or Bar Esquina after.

April 17 Art Walk is your first night. Don't miss it.

Mountain escape

Sierra de la Laguna day hike

~45 min drive into the Sierra

A half-day hike into the mountains that rise behind the Corridor — oak and pine forest above 1,500m, condors, ocelot country, and temperatures 10°C cooler than the coast. April is the last good month before the approach dries out entirely. Hire a local guide in Todos Santos for a combined weekend-trip hike, or drive up from the Corridor independently.

04 · The Weekend

La Paz & Todos Santos — the two-day Baja circuit

The right call for your long-run weekend: combine Todos Santos (1.5 hrs north on Hwy 19 — Baja's surf-and-art town) with La Paz (3 hrs from Los Cabos) for whale sharks in the sea. April 17 is your arrival Thursday; your first full weekend is April 19–20. Go early, while April whale shark availability lasts.

Whale shark season alert: La Paz whale shark season officially runs October through early April. Some operators extend to mid-April in good conditions — others close March 31. Call Baja Whale Shark or Cabo Trek directly when you arrive April 17 to confirm availability for April 19. If season has closed, Todos Santos alone is an excellent weekend.

Drive Highway 19 — it's one of the great Baja roads

Highway 19 north from Cabo San Lucas to Todos Santos runs along the Pacific coast through farmland, desert, and surf beaches. A private driver is the move: the road is good but the Pacific views demand your attention, not the wheel. Stop at Playa Los Cerritos (a long, flat beach) for a look before Todos Santos. Rent a car only if you want full flexibility on the La Paz leg.

The shape of the weekend — April 19–20

Saturday early morning — The long run Run from your Corridor hotel before departure. Malecón San José (flat, 5–6 mi loop), or Medano Beach at low tide for 8–10 miles on sand. Target 5:30am start for a 16+ mile session before the car. Carry 40+ oz of fluid; the Baja air will take it. Then shower, eat, and drive north.
Saturday AM — Drive to Todos Santos 1.5 hours on Hwy 19. Stop at Playa Los Cerritos (a long surf beach, beautiful even in April) on the way in. Arrive Todos Santos by 10am.
Saturday in Todos Santos The Hotel California (yes, that one), the Art District galleries, El Mercadito food market for lunch, El Tri Coffee for a slow afternoon flat white. The town is genuinely charming — whitewashed, artsy, cool for the desert. Stay overnight at Hotel Guaycura or the Todos Santos Inn if you want the full experience.
Sunday — La Paz whale sharks (if season open) Drive from Todos Santos to La Paz (2 hrs). Morning boat tour with Baja Whale Shark or similar — 2–3 hours in the water with whale sharks in the Bay of La Paz, the gentle giants that can reach 12 meters. Return south on Hwy 1 to Los Cabos by evening. The drive back is stunning at sunset through the Baja desert.
If whale sharks are closed — full Todos Santos Spend Sunday morning at Playa Punta Lobos (surf beach, dramatic blowhole cliffs) and hike the El Faro lighthouse trail (2 hrs, Pacific views, manageable for a recovery-day effort). Drive back via Pescadero and Cerritos. Still an excellent weekend — Todos Santos doesn't need La Paz to be the right call.

Long-run terrain note: Todos Santos and the surrounding coast offers runnable flat beach road from town toward Cerritos — quiet, scenic, low traffic, cool morning air off the Pacific. Sea-level, no altitude adjustment. An 18–22 mile out-and-back is entirely feasible before 9am. The alternative on a weekend-only long run: the Malecón San José pre-departure loop is flat, paved, and adds up fast.

Art, design & the marina promenade

Best destination

San José del Cabo Gallery District

Art District · SJdC centro

About 20 high-end galleries showcasing original paintings, sculpture, photography, handcrafted jewelry, and investment-grade indigenous art. The best galleries — Galería de Ida Victoria, Galería Los Cabos, Arte Baja — mix established Mexican artists with international contemporary work. Go on Thursday for the Art Walk (5–9pm, Oct–June), or during the day when you can linger with gallery owners.

You arrive on a Thursday — the Art Walk is your first evening. Plan dinner in the Art District after.

Marina luxury

Boulevard Marina & Medano Strip

CSL Marina promenade

The marina promenade runs the length of the waterfront with jewelry stores, upscale swimwear boutiques, leather goods, and high-quality Cuban cigar shops. Ultrafemme and Ultima Joya carry quality watches and fine jewelry. Not a mall — an open-air luxury strip. Go in the late afternoon when the light hits the water and browse without a destination.

Independent

Sur Baja Mercado

SJdC

Curated artisan goods from Baja makers — hand-thrown pottery, woven textiles, natural cosmetics, small-batch hot sauces and mezcals. The edit-forward boutique that sources things you can't find at a marina souvenir shop. The Baja equivalent of a serious concept store.

Farm luxury

Flora Farms Shop

Sierra foothills · SJdC

Organic products, branded farm goods, artisan Baja pantry items, and the best selection of local mezcal and wine in one curated shop. Buy olive oil, local honey, and a bottle of Baja nebbiolo when you're there for dinner. The gift that actually says where you were.

Agave

Specialty tequila & mezcal boutiques

Marina area · CSL

Several serious agave-spirits shops near the marina carry expressions you can't find in the US — small-batch mezcals from Oaxaca, aged reposados, limited artisanal runs. Mexican customs allows bringing bottles back. Los Cabos tequila shops are well-curated by design — the tourist market demands quality here. Ask for the in-house sommelier's pick.

Hotel California

Todos Santos boutiques

Todos Santos · weekend trip

On the weekend trip: Todos Santos' main street and art district has excellent small boutiques — Mexican textile designers, silver jewelry makers, surfer-artisan clothing. The Hotel California gift shop is actually good. Bring cash; many small shops don't take cards reliably.

07 · The Pantry

Whole-Foods-style groceries

For cooking-at-the-villa nights, Los Cabos has a surprisingly strong premium grocery infrastructure — La Comer (the region's Whole Foods equivalent) has two accessible locations, and there's a dedicated organic grocer and a good gourmet Saturday market in SJdC.

Stores confirmed operating as of June 2026. La Comer is the headline choice — consistently stocked, excellent quality, Whole Foods comparison earns its way.
Main shop

La Comer — San José del Cabo

Paseo Malecon San Jose S/N · SJdC · open 7am–11pm

The premium supermarket that replaced Mega and redefined grocery shopping in Los Cabos. Wide aisles, excellent produce section, strong organic and health-food department, seafood counter with octopus, tuna steaks, and fresh lobster, in-house bakery, wine wall. Reviewers call it "Whole Foods quality in Baja." The main weekly shop stop.

La Comer — Palmilla / Corridor

Carretera Mexico 1, Palmilla · Corridor

The second La Comer location, more convenient if you're staying in a Corridor resort. Same quality and selection as the SJdC flagship. Particularly useful for a grocery run after returning from Cabo Pulmo or another full-day excursion — open late.

Organic-focused

Supermarket Santa Carmela

CSL and SJdC locations

The dedicated organic grocer in Los Cabos, operating since 2000. Organic produce, imported health foods, clean pantry staples, natural cleaning products. A smaller-footprint store with higher organic percentage than La Comer — the move for a health-food-focused shop or when you want to be confident about sourcing.

California Ranch

California Ranch Market

Corridor / SJdC

The expat community's grocery institution — US-style selection with imported brands, excellent deli counter, good wine and beer section. Well-stocked with the familiar-from-home items that La Comer sometimes misses. The "safe" choice when you need exactly what you're looking for.

californiaranchmarket.com — also does grocery delivery to Corridor resorts.

Saturday morning

El Merkado — Saturday market

Art District · SJdC · Saturdays

The gourmet indoor market in the Art District opens Saturday morning with artisan food stalls alongside the permanent vendors. Fresh local tortillas, aged cheeses from Ensenada, Baja chorizos, seasonal produce, local honey, and the excellent coffee counter. Build your villa-cooking Saturday around a market visit.

Bulk & backup

Costco — San José del Cabo

SJdC

Yes, there's a Costco, and yes, it's very good. The Corridor expat community swears by it for quality-to-price on produce, protein, and pantry staples. Worth one visit for bulk purchases — wine, olive oil, protein, and the excellent bakery section — particularly for a longer stay with villa cooking.
