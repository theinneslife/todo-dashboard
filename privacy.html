Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 20, 2026

This privacy policy describes how personal data is collected and used in connection with personal productivity and training applications built and operated by Christopher Innes (“I,” “me,” or “my”).

1. Scope

This policy applies to personal applications that integrate with third-party services including, but not limited to, WHOOP, Strava, Google Calendar, and Gmail. These applications are for personal, non-commercial use only and are not distributed to third parties.

2. Data Collected

When connecting third-party services, the following types of data may be accessed:

  • WHOOP: Recovery scores, heart rate variability (HRV), resting heart rate, sleep data, and strain metrics.
  • Strava: Activity summaries, run distance, pace, elevation, and workout history (including private activities when authorized).
  • Google Calendar / Gmail: Calendar events and email content, used solely for personal scheduling and communication drafts.

3. How Data Is Used

All data accessed through these integrations is used exclusively for:

  • Personal training analysis and coaching recommendations
  • Displaying health and fitness metrics on a private personal dashboard
  • Automating personal scheduling and productivity workflows

Data is never sold, shared with third parties, or used for commercial purposes.

4. Data Storage

Data may be temporarily processed in memory during automated tasks. No health or fitness data is persisted to permanent storage beyond what is already stored within the originating third-party service (e.g., WHOOP or Strava).

5. Data Retention

No personal data is retained beyond the duration of a single session or automated task run. All integrations can be revoked at any time through the respective third-party service’s account settings.

6. Third-Party Services

This application connects to third-party APIs subject to their own privacy policies. You are encouraged to review the privacy policies of WHOOP, Strava, and Google for more information on how those services handle your data.

7. Security

OAuth tokens and credentials are used only within secure, authenticated sessions and are not stored in plain text or shared with any external parties.
