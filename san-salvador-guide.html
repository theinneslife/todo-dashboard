Orientation

What to know before you move

El Salvador runs on US dollars, has no altitude adjustment from sea level, and has undergone one of the most dramatic crime transformations in modern history. Context and tactics, up front.

Currency

USD

El Salvador officially dollarized in 2001. You will never convert a peso, exchange a colón, or do math on the street. Your card works, your cash works, exactly as at home.

Altitude

658 m

≈ 2,160 ft. No acclimatization needed — you came from Bogotá at 2,640 m. If anything, your lungs will feel loose and fast. July humidity is the only adjustable variable.

July Climate

28–32 °C

Rainy season: afternoons bring reliable downpours (usually 3–6 PM), mornings are dry and clear. Run before 6:30 AM. Heat index with humidity can push 37 °C by noon.

Getting Around

Uber

Uber works reliably in San Salvador and is the safe default. InDriver also available and sometimes cheaper. Avoid unmarked street taxis. Most restaurants in Escalón / San Benito are a $3–5 Uber.

Running in July

5:30 AM

Start before 6 AM to beat heat and humidity. Key routes: Colonia Escalón residential grid (rolling, quiet), Parque Cuscatlán loop, or drive 20 min to Parque Bicentenario in Antiguo Cuscatlán for long efforts. Electrolyte tabs mandatory. No altitude bonus here — it's all lungs and heat.

Origin

This is a producing country. Salvadoran Pacamara and Bourbon varieties regularly score 90+ at Cup of Excellence. The specialty scene in San Salvador is world-class — you're drinking coffee from 90 minutes away. Always ask for single-origin.

The Bukele transformation — what actually happened & what it means for you

  • El Salvador's homicide rate fell from 53 per 100,000 in 2018 to 1.8 per 100,000 in 2024 — a 98% reduction. Once dubbed the world's murder capital, it now ranks among the Western Hemisphere's safest countries.
  • In March 2022, Bukele declared a state of exception after gang violence spiked. Over 84,000 people were arrested, many held without due process or evidence — a human rights concern that persists.
  • The practical result for travelers: Zona Rosa, San Benito, and Escalón feel genuinely relaxed. Locals walk freely at night in ways that were unthinkable five years ago. Tourist numbers up 60% vs. 2019.
  • The US State Department downgraded its travel advisory in November 2024 due to decreased gang-related crime. Exercise normal caution in tourist zones.
  • Context matters: the transformation is real and dramatic; the methods remain contested. Keep normal big-city awareness — phone off the table, don't flash gear.
  • Escalón and San Benito are the most upscale residential / dining zones. You'll be comfortable walking both by day and most evenings.
  • El Tunco and La Libertad coast are busy tourist corridors — safe and well-trafficked by surfers and travelers.
  • Emergency line: 911. Tourist police active in major tourist zones. Embassy: US Embassy on Blvd. Santa Elena, Antiguo Cuscatlán.

Local dive · essential

A coffee origin you can drink in

El Salvador produces some of the world's most prized coffee — Pacamara and Bourbon varieties from farms at 1,400–1,800 m in Chalatenango and Santa Ana regularly score 90+ at Cup of Excellence. San Salvador's third-wave café scene is built entirely on domestic beans, 60–90 minutes from where they grew. You will not drink a better cup of origin coffee anywhere on the trip.

03 · The Afternoons

One neat experience a day

El Salvador is compact and road-accessible — a country you can cross in three hours. The afternoons listed here range from $0 to $50 and span volcano craters, art museums, colonial market towns, and the best pupusas of your life.

Signature · close

El Boquerón — crater hike

10 km from Escalón · 45 min round-trip hike

San Salvador volcano's collapsed crater sits 1,893 m above sea level, 10 km from your door. Walk the rim for views over the city and the Pacific beyond. A smaller nested crater inside is one of the most otherworldly sights in Central America. Free to enter the national park; go mornings before clouds roll in.

Start by 7 AM for clear summit views. Bring water — it's a dry, exposed climb.

Culture

MARTE — Museo de Arte de El Salvador

Colonia San Benito · 5-min Uber

San Salvador's leading contemporary and fine art museum in a handsome San Benito building. Rotating exhibitions trace Salvadoran visual culture from colonial through contemporary — artists grappling with migration, civil war memory, and national identity. Entry $3–5. Excellent small café and bookshop inside.

Check museomarte.org for current shows. Closed Mondays.

Day trip

Ruta de Las Flores

~1.5 hr west · full day

El Salvador's most scenic road trip: a chain of colonial coffee towns (Nahuizalco, Juayúa, Ataco) draped with bougainvillea and murals, at 1,200 m in the Apaneca mountain range. Weekend food festivals in Juayúa are outstanding. Nahuizalco is famous for rattan and wickerwork. Ataco has excellent craft markets and street art. Get a driver for the day — about $60–80 round-trip.

Go Saturday for the Juayúa food festival. Combine with Apaneca or Lago de Coatepeque.

Local

Mercado Nacional de Artesanías

Centro · 15-min Uber

El Salvador's premier artisan market — hammocks, carved masks, painted gourds, intricate textiles, handmade leather goods. The cafeteria inside is excellent and shockingly cheap for authentic comida típica. A solid two-hour afternoon browse with no obligation to spend much.

Coffee + farm

Coffee farm visit — Santa Ana highlands

~1 hr drive · half-day

Several farms near Santa Ana and Chalatenango offer visits during the off-season (July) to walk the trees and understand the micro-climate that produces 90-point Pacamara beans. Ask at Alquimia or Viva Espresso — they have direct relationships with growers and can arrange introductions or guided visits.

Off-season means green cherries on the tree — the harvest experience. Reserve ahead.

Essential · free

Pupusa crawl — Izalco road

Evenings, citywide

El Salvador's national dish eaten as it was meant to be eaten: at a plastic table, from a lady with a comal, for $0.50–1 each. Loroco-and-cheese (revuelta) is the move. Curtido — the fermented cabbage slaw — is non-negotiable. Ask your host or hotel for the nearest serious pupusería. This is the one meal you'll think about for years.

04 · The Weekend

El Tunco + Santa Ana Volcano: your long-run weekend

The Pacific coast is 45 minutes from Escalón. The Santa Ana Volcano is 2 hours. Combine them in a single Friday-out / Sunday-back weekend that includes the best surf beach in El Salvador, a 2,384 m active volcano hike, and your long run built directly into the experience.

Two destinations, one weekend

El Tunco is El Salvador's iconic surf village — the dark volcanic sand, the big rock, the consistent breaks that draw surfers from California and Europe. Santa Ana Volcano (Volcán Ilamatepec) at 2,384 m is El Salvador's highest peak, with a turquoise crater lake inside a nested caldera and views spanning Coatepeque Lake, Izalco Volcano, and the Pacific. A private driver ($100–130 for the weekend) connects both. Pick-up from El Tunco for the volcano is around 5:30 AM.

The shape of the weekend

Friday PM Uber or private driver to El Tunco (~45 min from Escalón). Check into a surf hostel or La Guitarra hotel. Dinner at La Bocana restaurant on the beach. Drinks at Roots Bar watching the Pacific sunset. July nights at sea level are warm and breezy.
Saturday AM — the long run Run at dawn (5:30 AM) before the heat hits — along the coastal highway toward El Sunzal or south toward El Zonte (Playa Bitcoin village). 10–16 miles possible on paved road. Sea level air, ocean breeze, volcanic black sand coastline, almost no traffic at that hour. Have your driver crew a water/gel drop at 8 miles. Cool down with a swim after. This is one of the more beautiful long run settings in Central America.
Saturday afternoon Recovery swim, cold shower, a proper beach lunch. El Tunco has good food — La Bocana and Tunco Surf Café are both solid. Afternoon: watch the surfers or take a lesson. The right break for beginners is visible from the beach. Brooke can surf while Christopher's legs recover.
Sunday 5:30 AM — Santa Ana Volcano Driver picks you up. ~2 hr drive to the trailhead near Cerro Verde National Park. The hike is 3 hrs round-trip (moderate difficulty, 1.5 hrs up, 1.5 hrs down) — paved path transitions to packed trail. Summit reward: a turquoise-blue active crater lake, views of Lago Coatepeque, Izalco Volcano (the "Lighthouse of the Pacific"), and on clear days, the Pacific. Tour guides available at the trailhead for ~$15; worth taking one.
Sunday PM Descend, stop at Lago Coatepeque for a swim and lunch at one of the lakeside restaurants. Drive back to San Salvador (1.5 hrs). Sunday evening: dinner at Monarca to wind down properly.

Booking the hike: Santa Ana Volcano requires a permit/guide reservation in advance through the national parks office (CORSATUR) or via a tour operator like Tunco Life or Eco Tours El Salvador. July availability is good but confirm 3–5 days ahead. Bring a layer — the summit is 2,384 m and windy even in July.

05 · Luxury Shopping

Multiplaza, La Gran Vía & Zona Rosa

San Salvador has a concentrated, well-curated luxury retail scene anchored by three distinct destinations — each with its own character, within 15 minutes of Escalón. Local artisan finds and Salvadoran designers are the unique plays here.

Premier mall

Multiplaza

Antiguo Cuscatlán · 15-min drive

San Salvador's most upscale contemporary mall — designer boutiques, premium international brands (Zara, Hugo Boss, premium anchors), multi-screen cinema, and an entire wing of the city's best lounges and restaurants. Air-conditioned, well-secured, the polished afternoon destination when the heat is at its worst (2–4 PM). Excellent food court options for a quick lunch.

Open-air lifestyle

La Gran Vía

Antiguo Cuscatlán · near Multiplaza

El Salvador's most elegant open-air shopping center — a European-style promenade with premium international retailers (Siman flagship, international fashion brands), excellent restaurants, a cinema, and regular pop-up markets featuring local artisans and designers. A genuinely lovely space to walk in the morning before it gets hot. Best coffee: the Viva Espresso here is excellent.

Cooler in the morning; the outdoor layout means evenings are also pleasant.

Boutique district

Zona Rosa — Blvd del Hipódromo

San Benito · walk/close Uber

The boutique strip along Boulevard del Hipódromo — independent fashion, jewelry, leather, and lifestyle shops in a more intimate setting than the malls. The Basilea shopping center here houses small jewelers and specialty boutiques. The right place for one-off pieces and local designer finds rather than international chains.

Salvadoran design

Siman Department Store

La Gran Vía + multiple

El Salvador's own premium department store — Central America's answer to Nordstrom. A mix of international brands and curated local lines. Strong cosmetics, home goods, and fashion floors. The place to pick up quality Salvadoran products in a polished environment.

Artisan & unique

Mercado Ex-Cuartel

Historic Center · 15-min Uber

Two floors of artisan stalls in a covered market in the historic center — hammocks, painted ceramics, handwoven textiles, carved wood, balsam products (El Salvador produces some of the world's finest balsam). Nothing here is mass-produced. The most authentic shopping in the city, and prices are negotiable.

Concept + coffee

Local designer boutiques — San Benito

Around Alquimia & MARTE

Several independent Salvadoran designers have opened boutiques in San Benito near the MARTE museum and Alquimia — quality leather goods, handmade jewelry, ethical fashion. Ask at your hotel or at Alquimia; the local design community is small, friendly, and proud to direct you.

07 · The Pantry

Whole-Foods-style groceries & health food

El Salvador's supermarket scene is anchored by Super Selectos and La Despensa de Don Juan, both with premium flagship stores near Escalón. For organic and health-food focused shopping, the specialty options are growing but more limited — plan ahead.

Note: El Salvador uses USD — no currency math required. Supermarket prices are comparable to mid-range US grocery stores. Quality fresh produce and imported goods are widely available in upscale locations.
Largest chain · closest

Super Selectos — Escalón flagship

Multiple Escalón & San Benito locations

El Salvador's largest supermarket chain (founded 1940, now the country's premier retailer). The Escalón and San Benito flagship stores are the premium format — wide aisles, in-store cafeteria, quality imported goods, excellent produce and deli sections. Open early and late. Their organic and health food section is expanding. The default first stop for everything.

Premium · Walmart-backed

La Despensa de Don Juan

17 locations citywide · Multiplaza + Escalón

Operated by Walmart Mexico y Centroamérica — 17 locations in El Salvador with a premium positioning strategy. Strong imported goods sections, reliable produce quality, good wine and spirits selection. The Multiplaza location is the flagship. Slightly more international product range than Super Selectos. Good for specialty ingredients you can't find elsewhere.

Budget · everywhere

Maxi Despensa

Citywide

The value-format sibling to La Despensa de Don Juan — good for basics, snacks, and run nutrition at non-premium pricing. When you just need a banana and electrolyte tablets at 5 AM, this is your stop.

Organic & health

Mercado Orgánico & natural food shops

San Benito / Escalón — ask locally

A small but growing organic food movement exists in San Salvador — several health food boutiques and small organic markets have opened in San Benito and Escalón. Ask at Pure 503 or Alquimia café for the current best referral; the health-conscious community in these neighborhoods knows exactly where to send you for clean produce, supplements, and natural products.

Verify location with your hotel concierge on arrival — the organic retail scene evolves quickly.

Farmers market

Weekend mercados — Santa Tecla & San Benito

Saturdays and Sundays

Santa Tecla's Mercado Municipal (15 min west) runs a genuine farmers-market atmosphere on weekends — local produce, artisan foods, fresh herbs, honey. The Santa Tecla food scene is San Salvador's Brooklyn — young chefs, good street food, artisan products. Worth a Saturday morning expedition.

Santa Tecla is safe and accessible by Uber — about $5 from Escalón.

Run nutrition

Sports nutrition — what to pack vs. source locally

Various

Bring your preferred gels and electrolyte tabs from home — the specialty sports nutrition market in El Salvador is limited. Super Selectos stocks Gatorade, basic nutrition bars, and some electrolyte powders. For long run days, stock up on bananas, dates, and coconut water (cheap and everywhere). Tropical fruits at any market are your best natural fuel.
