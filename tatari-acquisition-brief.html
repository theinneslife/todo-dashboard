What Tatari Does: Three Integrated Business Lines
📺

Tatari (Demand Tech)

Tatari, Inc.  |  FEIN: 81-3734795

The core platform. AI-enabled, performance-native CTV media buying across Linear, Streaming, and Online Video. Provides brands and agencies full transparency into pricing and inventory. Operates as a DSP-like interface but with direct publisher integrations rather than relying on programmatic auctions. 300+ advertiser clients. Services brands direct and also licenses the platform to ~100 independent agencies (launched late 2022; agencies now 9% of net revenue). Clients funnel from Growth (under $2M media) through Mid-Size ($2M-$10M) to Enterprise ($10M+). Enterprise = 10 clients but 34% of net revenue.

⚡

Upstream TV (Supply Tech)

Upstream TV, Inc.  |  FEIN: 85-1296931

A next-generation DSP/supply mediation layer. Buys remnant impressions in bulk (approx. $10 CPM run-of-site), enriches them with Tatari's household data, AI audience models, and contextual signals, then resells to Tatari clients at approximately $20 CPM, generating a $2 per thousand impression margin. 60M+ impressions per day. No programmatic tax. Enables 67% more impressions for the same budget versus standard programmatic buys. Warner Bros. Discovery described it: "[Tatari] is roughly 3 years ahead of the market." Just entering meaningful scale: $1.8M net revenue in 2025, projecting $5M in 2026 and $20.8M by 2027.

🔐

Vault Ad Solutions (Data + Measurement)

Vault Ad Solutions, Inc.  |  FEIN: 93-1723392

Deterministic, neutral, privacy-native identity and attribution across the Convergent TV spectrum. Proprietary TV device graph built over 8+ years. 100M+ household events ingested daily. Data partnerships with Experian, Comscore, Samba TV, Nielsen, Kantar, Vizio, Oracle. Enables true household-level measurement (not panel-based estimates), closed-loop attribution, next-day results, and multi-dimensional analysis (publishers, time, segments, incrementality). Current revenue is minimal (sub $1M), but it is the data moat that powers everything else and is a potential standalone licensing business.

Why Three? Are They Integrated?

The three entities are legally separate but operationally a single flywheel. Tatari drives demand and client relationships. Vault's device graph and measurement layer provides the data advantage that justifies Tatari's performance claims. Upstream uses Vault's identity data to intelligently route and enrich supply, then routes inventory back to Tatari's demand side. The integration is the moat: no other competitor in CTV has all three layers owned and operating in a closed loop. The "three business lines" structure is partly a GP Bullhound presentation device to show growth vectors beyond the core DSP platform, and partly real: Upstream and Vault are separately incorporated subsidiaries, each with independent revenue trajectories.

Why Take Rate Expands Significantly (12.6% to 18%)

  • Upstream scales from $0.3M (2025) to $32.2M (2028) net revenue, all incremental high-margin supply tech income on top of Tatari fees
  • Client mix shifts toward Enterprise and Agency (higher rates, lower churn)
  • PAO (Performance-as-Outcome) clients pay on outcomes, not CPMs, driving higher effective take rates
  • Vault data licensing revenues begin to scale independently
  • Agency platform licenses (launched late 2022) now 9% of net revenue and growing
The take-rate expansion from 12.6% to 18% over 3 years is the most important financial story here. It means Tatari's revenue can grow faster than gross media volume even in a flat spend environment.

Strategic Fit with MNTN: Synergies, Gaps Filled, and Conflicts

Linear TV Access: MNTN's Biggest Gap

MNTN is a streaming-only platform. Tatari generates $435M of gross media in linear TV annually (63.6% of its total). Acquiring Tatari gives MNTN immediate access to the $47B national linear and $22B local linear markets where it currently has zero presence. Convergent TV buyers increasingly need one platform for both. This is the single most compelling strategic argument for the deal.

Proprietary Device Graph Amplifies MNTN Measurement

Vault's 8-year, 100M+ event-per-day device graph is a genuine data asset MNTN does not have. Embedding this into MNTN's measurement and attribution layer would significantly improve frequency capping, cross-screen attribution, and household-level reporting across MNTN's existing CTV client base, without building it from scratch.

Upstream Supply Tech: Economics for MNTN's Buy Side

Tatari's Upstream product buys impressions at $10 CPM and resells at $20 CPM, capturing the $2/CPM spread. This supply mediation layer could be extended to MNTN's CTV inventory, potentially improving unit economics for MNTN clients and generating incremental high-margin revenue. MNTN currently relies on third-party supply (DSP auctions). Owning the supply layer is a meaningful structural advantage.

Client Cross-Sell: Significant Overlap in DTC Verticals

Both companies serve performance-oriented DTC brands (health, fintech, apparel, consumer services). Tatari clients would be natural upsells to MNTN's streaming capabilities. MNTN clients would benefit from Tatari's linear buying. The combined platform could credibly offer "all of TV" to a shared DTC audience, significantly expanding wallet share per client.

Agency Revenue Stream: New Distribution for MNTN

Tatari launched an agency licensing model in late 2022. It now has 100 agencies on the platform generating 9% of net revenue. MNTN has minimal agency distribution. Tatari's agency network is a meaningful new distribution channel MNTN could leverage immediately post-acquisition.

Engineering Talent Acquisition: 98 Engineers

~98 engineers (29.9% of headcount) focused on Publisher Platform, Buying and Optimization, and Reporting and Measurement. The Data Science team (~38 people) built the device graph and measurement tech over 8 years. This is deep, hard-to-replicate institutional knowledge. Key risk: retention post-acquisition requires thoughtful incentive design.

Potential Product Conflict: CTV Overlap

Both MNTN and Tatari offer CTV buying and measurement. CTV is 36.4% of Tatari's gross media and growing fast. There will be overlap in streaming inventory access, measurement methodology, and client-facing product. A combined entity would need a clear product integration roadmap to avoid confusion and client churn. This is a manageable integration challenge, not a dealbreaker, but needs a plan before close.

Culture and Founder Risk

Philip Inghelbrecht holds 40% voting control and is active CEO. Michael Swinson (Chief Data Scientist) holds 30% voting. Both need to be retained for at least 2-3 years post-close. The data science IP (Vault, Upstream AI models) lives largely in Swinson and his team. Losing either founder within 12 months of close would be damaging. Deal structure must include multi-year employment agreements and meaningful equity roll.

Valuation Framework
Indicative Acquisition Range (Net Revenue Multiple)
$400M to $600M
Based on 2026P net revenue of $111.3M at 3.5x to 5.5x. Midpoint: ~$500M (~4.5x NTM revenue). MNTN has $210M+ cash, giving substantial capacity for all-cash or cash/equity structure.
Bear Case ($350M to $400M)

3.0-3.5x 2026P NTM revenue. Justified if gross media growth disappoints, take-rate expansion stalls, or 2025's flat gross media (-1.1%) signals structural linear headwinds. Equivalent to 47-55x 2025 EBITDA.

Base Case ($450M to $550M)

4.0-5.0x 2026P NTM revenue. Reflects take-rate expansion story, Upstream growth optionality, device graph IP, and a clean balance sheet with no institutional debt. Management projections show $190.9M revenue and $37M EBITDA by 2028.

Bull Case ($550M to $700M)

5.0-6.0x NTM revenue. Justified if Upstream scales to $20M+ quickly and the combined entity can credibly market "all of Convergent TV" to MNTN's existing base, plus strategic scarcity premium for the only profitable full-stack CTV operator in market.

Deal Structure Thoughts

Prefer all-cash or majority-cash given MNTN's $210M cash position and access to debt markets. Earnout component tied to 2027 revenue milestones ($149.7M target) could align interests and reduce upfront price risk. Equity rollover for founders (20-25% of consideration) preferred for retention.

Comparable transactions: Innovid went public at ~$1.3B in 2021 on ~$120M revenue (10x+). VideoAmp raised at ~$1.4B (speculative, money-losing). Tatari is more profitable than either. However, the private market has repriced adtech significantly since 2021. A 4-5x NTM net revenue multiple on a profitable, growing platform is a realistic negotiating anchor. GP Bullhound will likely anchor to 6-8x; MNTN should anchor to 3.5-4.5x and meet in the middle.
Key Risks and Open Questions
High
Linear TV Structural Decline
Linear gross media declined from $488M (2024) to $435M (2025), down 10.9% YoY. Linear is still 63.6% of gross media. If cord-cutting accelerates, Tatari's largest revenue segment shrinks. The company is betting that linear inventory becomes more valuable as it becomes scarcer (fewer eyeballs = higher CPMs and more premium placement) and that it can move clients "up the stack" to streaming. Tatari's own projections assume linear declines modestly. Validate this assumption in diligence.
High
Enterprise Client Concentration
10 Enterprise clients represent 34% of net revenue. Losing 2-3 Enterprise clients post-acquisition (disruption fear, competitive pressure, or key-person departure) could materially impair revenue. Need the top 10 client list, contract terms, and renewal history before signing LOI.
High
Founder Retention: Inghelbrecht and Swinson
Philip Inghelbrecht is the operating CEO. Michael Swinson built the data science foundation that underpins Vault and Upstream. Both hold significant voting control and equity. If either walks within 18 months of close, the acquirer faces real risk of IP erosion and culture damage. Multi-year employment agreements with meaningful retention equity are non-negotiable in any deal.
Med
2026 Revenue Acceleration Assumption
Net revenue growth was 7.1% in 2025 (slowed significantly from 31.5% in 2024). Management projects 28.6% in 2026 driven by Upstream scaling and take-rate expansion. This is a meaningful acceleration that requires validation. The $19.3M current pipeline ($10.9M weighted) supports near-term growth but does not fully bridge the implied $24.7M revenue step-up. Ask for monthly revenue run-rates Q1 2026 to confirm the trajectory.
Med
Accounts Payable: $103.4M vs. Receivables $55.8M
The balance sheet shows $103.4M in accounts payable (Q1 2026) vs. $55.8M in receivables. AP significantly exceeds AR, which is normal for a media-buying business (Tatari pays publishers before it collects from clients), but the working capital deficit needs to be modeled carefully in deal structure. The $201M in cash/securities is more than adequate coverage, but confirm timing of cash cycles in diligence.
Med
Product Integration Complexity
MNTN's CTV platform and Tatari's CTV platform overlap on streaming inventory access, measurement, and client-facing UX. A combined entity needs a 12-18 month product integration roadmap before close. Risk: clients of both platforms are confused, or the CTV products cannibalize each other before integration is complete. Key question: does Tatari become MNTN's linear layer while MNTN remains the CTV lead, or do they fully merge into a single convergent TV platform?
Med
Geographic Talent Risk: Ukraine and Eastern Europe
The employee census shows engineering talent in Ukraine (Zaporizhzhia, Kyiv, Vovchynetske village) and Czechia. Given geopolitical instability in Ukraine, there is operational risk in this workforce. Quantify the Ukraine engineering headcount as part of diligence and assess business continuity plans.
Low
Vault Revenue Still Minimal
Vault Ad Solutions (privacy/identity data) has sub-$1M current revenue despite 8+ years of asset building. The device graph is real and used internally, but standalone licensing monetization has not been proven. This is upside optionality, not a revenue-to-be-acquired. Do not pay for Vault as a standalone revenue business; treat it as a data infrastructure asset.
Low
Financial Statements are Unaudited
All financials in the VDR are explicitly marked unaudited. Revenue recognition practices (gross vs. net treatment of media buys, Upstream accounting) need audit-quality review before any LOI is signed. Deferred revenue of $85.2M (Q1 2026) should be understood in terms of timing and contractual obligations.
Acquisition Recommendation
Recommendation: Actively Explore / Proceed to LOI
The strategic case is compelling. Tatari fills MNTN's single most important capability gap: access to linear TV. No other asset in the market gives MNTN immediate, profitable, at-scale linear TV buying capability with a fully developed technology stack. The Tatari platform touches $687M of gross media, serves 300+ brands, has 13 granted patents, and generates positive EBITDA at an 83%+ gross margin. The device graph (Vault) and supply mediation layer (Upstream) are genuine moats with no direct competitive equivalent.

The financial profile is unusually clean for a transaction of this size: no institutional debt, no VC liquidation preferences, founder-controlled, profitable, with $201M in cash and securities on the balance sheet. This reduces deal complexity significantly.

The critical path: (1) Obtain Q1 2026 monthly revenue run-rates to validate the 2026 growth re-acceleration. (2) Obtain the top 10 Enterprise client list with contract terms and renewal history. (3) Negotiate multi-year retention agreements with Inghelbrecht and Swinson as a prerequisite to LOI. (4) Commission a product integration scoping exercise to determine how Tatari's CTV layer and MNTN's CTV layer get consolidated. (5) Run the Ukraine engineering headcount analysis.

Price anchor: Open negotiations at $400M-$450M (3.5-4x 2026P NTM revenue), walk away above $650M (6x). The most likely deal lands in the $480M-$560M range with an earnout of up to $50M tied to 2027 revenue milestones, plus equity rollover for both founders at 20% of total consideration. MNTN's $210M cash covers roughly half the deal; the remainder would require modest leverage or stock issuance.

One important flag: The 2025 net revenue growth of 7.1% (down from 31.5% in 2024) is a yellow flag. Before advancing to full diligence, confirm this is a deliberate transition year rather than evidence of market saturation or competitive pressure. The projections show a sharp recovery, but those projections come from the seller.
